## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

New Hampshire's injection molding landscape is significantly more substantial than its 29-row CWP footprint suggests. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep proved transformational, uncovering 12 injection molding operations — more than the CWP confirmed. The state hosts approximately 30 injection molding and mold-making operations, with **medical device manufacturing as the dominant end market** — a direct function of proximity to Boston's medtech corridor. New Hampshire's no-income-tax, no-sales-tax environment has attracted a cluster of ISO 13485-certified, cleanroom-equipped custom molders concentrated in the Nashua–Hudson–Milford–Pelham southern corridor.

**Key findings:**
- **4 High Priority** prospects with multiple strong readiness signals
- **7 Qualified** prospects worth monitoring or engaging
- **4 Watch List** captive/corporate operations
- **14 Excluded** (non-molders, chemical companies, consultancies, suppliers, wire manufacturer)
- **1 major PE acquisition:** Vance Street Capital acquired MRPC (Hudson) in October 2025 — within the 6–18 month optimal receptivity window
- **1 facility closure feeding consolidation:** Poly-Ject Inc. (Amherst) ceased injection molding May 2025; customers transferred to Advantage Plastic Products
- **CWP deduplication:** W.K. Hillquist (Hudson) was acquired by Granite State Plastics in August 2017. These two CWP entries are now one company.
- **Zero RJG/cavity pressure monitoring** explicitly advertised by any NH molder — a potential adoption gap
- **Top prospect:** MRPC (Hudson) — Vance Street Capital PE acquisition Oct 2025, ISO 13485, MedAccred injection molding accreditation, silicone micro-molding, PEEK for implantables. The "PE acquisition + medical device + multi-material molding" trifecta.
- **Top CWP prospect:** Granite State Plastics (Hudson) — 26 presses, ISO 13485, cleanroom, 3D-printed conformal cooling inserts, in-house tooling, absorbed W.K. Hillquist
- **Strongest beyond-CWP discovery:** TechNH (Nashua) — ITAR + ISO 13485 + Class 7 cleanroom + scientific molding. Exceptionally rare triple certification.
- **Active consolidation cycle:** Poly-Ject → APP customer transfer; GSP absorbed Hillquist; GI Plastek merged into PSI; Precision Tool & Die acquired by Carclo. Small NH molders are consolidating.
- **Hudson, NH is the densest molding cluster:** MRPC, Granite State Plastics, Proto Part, and formerly W.K. Hillquist — all within one small town.

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

**NH medical molding density is Tier 2-equivalent.** The concentration of ISO 13485-certified, cleanroom-equipped medical device molders in the Nashua–Hudson–Milford–Pelham belt rivals states with 3–4x the CWP entry count. This is a function of proximity to Boston's medtech corridor — many NH medical molders serve the same OEM customer base as MA molders, but with NH's tax advantages.

**The MRPC acquisition is the most time-sensitive opportunity in the state.** Vance Street Capital's October 2025 purchase places this within the optimal 6–18 month PE receptivity window through approximately April 2027. MRPC's MedAccred accreditation and silicone micro-molding capabilities represent the pinnacle of process complexity in NH.

**Hudson, NH is a micro-cluster.** MRPC, Granite State Plastics, and Proto Part all operate injection molding in one small town. A single site visit could address multiple prospects. The former W.K. Hillquist facility (now GSP) adds historical density.

**The zero-RJG gap creates both challenge and opportunity.** No NH molder publicly advertises RJG or cavity pressure monitoring. TechNH's scientific molding practice may use it without publicizing. This represents either a technology gap the alliance could fill or a market where in-mold sensing hasn't penetrated — either way, worth understanding through discovery conversations.

**Retiring expertise risk is acute at multiple companies.** Bill Blank at APP (40+ years mold design), the Mills family at PSI/GI Plastek (founding family in PE-transformed company), the Riemer family at MRPC (100+ years of family ownership ending with PE sale), and small operations like Concept Tool and Design (6 employees, 35 years of concentrated knowledge) all face knowledge preservation challenges.
