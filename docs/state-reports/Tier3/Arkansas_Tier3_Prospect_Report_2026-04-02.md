### 1.1 FM Corporation
**Location:** Rogers, AR (NW Arkansas corridor)
**CWP Contacts:** 2 — President/CEO Michael Watts, VP Operations Keith Poor
**Source:** CWP + Web Research

FM Corporation is a privately held, independent custom injection molder operating 13 presses (90–1,450 tons) in a 165,000 sq ft ISO 9001:2015-certified facility. Revenue approximately $30M with ~60 employees. Specializes in structural foam molding alongside high-pressure injection — a relatively uncommon dual capability. Every press has robotic part removal. Quality lab runs Zeiss and Brown & Sharpe CMMs. Secondary operations include ultrasonic welding, in-house painting, silk screening, and EMI/RFI shielding. Serves medical, defense, scientific, and telecom markets.

**Why High Priority:** Right size ($30M), right structure (independent, no corporate hierarchy), right process maturity (ISO, CMM, robotics but no advanced process monitoring — clear technology gap), and direct executive access via CWP (CEO + VP Ops). Cleanest single prospect in the state.

### 1.3 Premier Technical Plastics (PTP)
**Location:** Searcy, AR (+ Del Rio, TX facility)
**CWP Contacts:** 1 — Alan Freeland, Engineering Manager
**Source:** CWP (listed as "Rosti") + Web Research

**🚨 CRITICAL IDENTITY CORRECTION:** The CWP "Rosti Searcy" entry is actually Premier Technical Plastics (PTP), owned by McCalmont Industries LLC. PTP shares a historical lineage with Rosti — both descend from Rosti Technical Plastics under A.P. Moller-Maersk. In 2010, when Maersk sold non-US operations to Nordstjernan AB (creating today's Rosti Group Sweden), the US plants were excluded. Jim McCalmont led a management buyout of the three US facilities, rebranding them Premier Technical Plastics. The current Rosti Group subsequently entered the US market in 2022 by acquiring Plastic Components Inc. (the Clearfield, UT facility with $1.2M in RJG eDart). **These are completely separate corporate entities since 2010, and no RJG technology transfer occurred.**

PTP operates an estimated 40–70 presses (28–1,500 tons) across Searcy, AR and Del Rio, TX facilities. Approximately 200 employees, 200,000+ sq ft of manufacturing space. ISO certified, veteran-owned. Actively investing in two-shot injection molding capacity. Also offers gas-assist molding, insert molding, overmolding, in-mold labeling, and pad printing. Serves automotive, medical, consumer, electronics, and industrial markets.

**Why High Priority:** The absence of RJG is actually an opportunity — PTP has the scale and technical sophistication to benefit from cavity pressure monitoring but apparently lacks it. Two-shot investment signals willingness to adopt advanced technology. Owner-operated structure (McCalmont) enables fast decisions. Multi-facility operations create complexity where AI adds value.

**Engagement path:** Alan Freeland (Engineering Manager, CWP). **Must use "Premier Technical Plastics" name — NOT "Rosti."**

### 1.5 Colson Monette
**Location:** Monette, AR (NE Arkansas)
**CWP Contacts:** 0 — Beyond-CWP discovery
**Source:** Beyond-CWP Web Research (Thomasnet, Colson Group corporate)

Colson Monette operates 127 injection molding presses (30–1,500 tons) plus injection rubber presses (400–600 tons) in a 90,000 sq ft facility. Ships over 5.5 million parts annually on a 24/5 schedule. Operational since 1976. Division of Colson Group → Marmon Group → Berkshire Hathaway. Primary products: casters, wheels, rolls, hinges. **Why High Priority:** Largest single-site injection molding operation identified in Arkansas (127 presses). Servo-driven machines indicate some modernization, but no RJG or scientific molding signals — clear technology gap. In-house mold design/building adds tooling optimization value. Despite Berkshire Hathaway ownership, Marmon portfolio companies operate with relative autonomy. Key contacts: Bishop Sparks (Plant Manager), Jack Birdno (Plant Engineer).
