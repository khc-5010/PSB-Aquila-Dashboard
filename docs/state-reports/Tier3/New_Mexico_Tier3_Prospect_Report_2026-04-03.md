## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### Defense/Lab Supply Chain Gap
New Mexico has massive institutional demand for precision plastic components (Sandia National Labs, Los Alamos National Lab, Kirtland AFB, White Sands Missile Range, Holloman AFB) but essentially zero qualified local injection molding capacity. The only confirmed out-of-state defense molder serving Sandia is Rakar Inc. (Oxnard, CA — AS9100D, ITAR). VAMCO is the only local molder claiming lab market service, but has no visible certifications. Any NM injection molder investing in AS9100 or ITAR capability would face virtually zero local competition. This is a structural market gap.

### Intel/Semiconductor Supply Chain
Intel's Rio Rancho fab (Fab 11X) imports plastics among its top commodity categories. Desert Plastics (rotomolder) is the only confirmed local plastics supplier producing ultra-pure water vessels for the fab. Several NM machine shops serve Intel (Butterman Tool, Pro-Fab Inc.) but none are injection molders. Semiconductor-grade plastic components (wafer carriers, fluid handling, cleanroom accessories) are being sourced from out-of-state.

### Contact Departure Risk
Both CWP contacts may have departed their companies. Jim Terney (ACIM) appears on LinkedIn as now working at UbeCube LLC as Injection Molding Operations Manager. Anthony Menicucci (AMT) may be connected to Sandia National Labs rather than AMT. Michelle Hartmann should be notified for CWP cleanup.

### NM Is the Thinnest Plastics State Yet
With only 5 confirmed injection molding operations statewide (and one of uncertain status), New Mexico has the thinnest injection molding capacity of any state analyzed so far. IQS Directory lists zero NM injection molding companies. The state's manufacturing base is dominated by defense R&D, energy, and semiconductor fabrication — not traditional plastics processing.

### Saegertown/Meadville PA Connection
Multi-Plastics of New Mexico was a satellite of Multi-Plastics Inc. of Saegertown, PA — a company in the Meadville plastics cluster that was analyzed in the Tier 1 Meadville report. The PA entity no longer exists, but the NM facility may still operate. This is an unexpected cross-tier connection.
