## NOTES

### CWP exclusion rate analysis
CT CWP: 70 rows → ~52 unique companies → **22 excluded** (~42% exclusion rate). The exclusion profile is unique among Tier 3 states: the dominant noise source is **equipment OEMs and industry organizations** (Wittmann Battenfeld, Arburg, Davis-Standard, DRI-AIR, Branson/Emerson, SPE, All Things Bakelite) rather than the captive manufacturing OEMs that drive exclusions in most other states. This reflects Connecticut's historical role as the birthplace of the American plastics industry and the US headquarters for major European equipment suppliers. The equipment OEM presence is actually a positive ecosystem signal — their local customers are the custom molders in this pipeline.

### Geographic clusters — engagement recommendations
Four actionable clusters emerged:

1. **Manchester cluster** (ABA-PGT + Vision Technical Molding + Advance Mold & Manufacturing): Three companies within a few miles on Utopia Road and surrounding area. ABA-PGT and Vision are both ESOPs/employee-focused with medical device capability. A half-day visit could cover both plus Advance Mold's tool shop. Estimated 100+ machines combined.

2. **Naugatuck Valley / Oxford corridor** (PTA Plastics + Stelray + NPI Medical): PTA Plastics in Oxford is the #1 target; Stelray in Ansonia is 15 minutes south; NPI Medical (Westfall Technik) is also in Ansonia. Deep plastics heritage in the "Polymer Valley" region.

3. **Middletown corridor** (Plastic Design International + Prototype & Plastic Mold Co.): Two complementary companies — one molder, one mold maker — in the same city. Combined engagement leverage.

4. **Watertown cluster** (Forum Contract Manufacturing + Watertown Plastics): Forum's new facility and Watertown Plastics share the same town. Different scales but proximity enables efficient engagement.

### ESOP ownership pattern
Connecticut continues the ESOP pattern observed in Tennessee (4 TN targets). Two of the top 4 CT HP targets are 100% ESOP-owned: **PTA Plastics** (since 2012) and **ABA-PGT** (since 1988). Both have publicly emphasized data-driven operations and employee development. The ESOP framing — "every efficiency gain benefits your employee-owners" — is highly effective for these targets.

### PE ownership concentration
Three companies in the CT pipeline are PE-backed: **Forum Contract Manufacturing** (Squadron Capital, 2016), **NPI Medical** (Westfall Technik/Lee Equity, 2017), and **Albea/Eyelematic** (PAI Partners, 2018). Forum is the strongest PE prospect — Squadron's medical device focus and the brand-new facility create a clear value-creation narrative for AI adoption.

### Osterman & Company — channel partner opportunity
Osterman was reclassified from prospect to excluded (resin distributor), but their 3 CWP contacts (President, VP, Sales Manager) and $190M+ revenue transacting 1 billion+ pounds of resin annually makes them one of the largest distributors in New England. Their processor customer list is a warm introduction channel. Worth preserving as a strategic networking contact rather than a prospect.

### ITW Deltar — technology benchmark
While excluded as a captive OEM (Illinois Tool Works division), the Lakeville CT facility running 39 Wittmann Battenfeld lines with 22 employees in near-lights-out production is the most advanced Industry 4.0 injection molding operation in the state. Their Wittmann 4.0 connectivity and automation levels serve as a technology benchmark for what the alliance's AI platform could enable at smaller custom molders.
