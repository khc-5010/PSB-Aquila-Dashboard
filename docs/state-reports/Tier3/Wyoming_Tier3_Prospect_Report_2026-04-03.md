## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### Wyoming Plastics Landscape Assessment
- **Total confirmed injection molding operations in Wyoming:** 3 (Legacy Molding, Y-Tex, Central Custom Molding) + 1 unverified (Allsop). Estimated statewide injection molding workforce: **<75 people**.
- **Zero mold/tool shops identified.** Regional tool-and-die directory explicitly states: "We list no tool and die makers in this State." Any Wyoming manufacturer needing tooling goes to Colorado, Utah, Idaho, or further.
- **Zero RJG, cavity-pressure, or Moldflow usage statewide.** Wyoming is a complete technology desert for advanced process monitoring — consistent with Montana assessment.
- **Zero ISO-certified injection molders found.** No ISO 9001, ISO 13485, IATF 16949, or AS9100 certifications identified at any Wyoming injection molder despite Legacy Molding and CCM claiming medical/defense work.
- **Dominant plastics operation is pipe extrusion:** W.L. Plastics (Casper and Gillette) is Wyoming's largest plastics manufacturer — HDPE pipe extruder serving energy sector. Entirely irrelevant to injection molding.
- **Thomasnet "Wyoming" listings heavily polluted by Chinese shell registrations:** 30 N Gould Street, Sheridan is a well-known registered-agent address used by Chinese mold makers (Beska Mold/Shenzhen, LS Hardware Tech/Dongguan) to create fake Wyoming business presence. Directory results require careful filtering.
- **Manufacturing Works (Wyoming MEP):** Director Rocky Case is actively pushing AI/ML integration across Wyoming manufacturing. Their CONNEX Wyoming marketplace database (launched September 2022) could surface additional manufacturers but requires registration. Worth noting for future reference.
- **Wyoming's injection molding exists because of two niches:** livestock agriculture (Y-Tex ear tags) and rural isolation creating demand for local custom work (Legacy Molding, CCM). Without these niche drivers, the state would have zero injection molding operations.
