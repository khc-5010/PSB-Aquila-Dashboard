## BEYOND-CWP SWEEP RESULTS

The following entities were identified and confirmed as NOT injection molders:

- **Min Plastics & Supply** (Honolulu, est. 1950) — Hawaii's largest plastics company. Strictly a distributor and sheet fabricator (acrylic, polycarbonate, HDPE, UHMW). Cuts, bends, and fabricates plastic sheet stock. Does not injection mold. 7 employees.
- **Multiple SEO-generated listings** — Several auto-generated directory pages (Plastic-InjectionMolding.com, Yellow Pages, Manta) falsely suggest Hawaii-based injection molding services. All trace to mainland or overseas companies using Hawaii as a geographic keyword for search engine optimization. Zero actual Hawaii-based injection molders exist behind these listings.

No injection molding entities of any kind were identified in Hawaii.

## NOTES

- Hawaii is the most geographically distant U.S. jurisdiction from PSB (~5,000 miles). Even if a molder existed, the Tier 3 bar would require extraordinary signals to justify engagement at this distance.
- Hawaii's manufacturing economy is dominated by food processing (coffee, macadamia nuts, pineapple), military/defense support (Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard), and construction materials.
- The island geography makes injection molding particularly uneconomic: resin pellets must ship by ocean freight from mainland suppliers, and finished parts must ship back. Transit times (5–7 days from West Coast ports) and shipping costs create insurmountable disadvantages vs. mainland molders.
- Hawaii's total manufacturing employment across all sectors is approximately 14,000 — smaller than many individual plastics companies in the pipeline.
