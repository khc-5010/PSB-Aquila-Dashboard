## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Oklahoma's custom injection molding sector is thin — roughly 15–20 active operations statewide — but contains one standout prospect and several high-signal beyond-CWP discoveries. The state's plastics landscape is shaped by two forces: (1) the Tulsa aerospace manufacturing corridor and (2) oil & gas industrial demand for specialty seals, connectors, and high-performance polymers.

**Gavco Plastics is the clear #1 target** — a family-owned (Gavlik family, since 1976) custom injection molder in Broken Arrow with 18 presses, AS9100/ISO 13485/ITAR certifications, in-house Moldflow analysis, full in-house tooling, cleanroom assembly, and recent Arburg Allrounder investments. The multi-industry complexity (aerospace, medical, automotive, food service, environmental) and engineering culture make this Oklahoma's strongest Tier 3 prospect by a wide margin.

**True Position Plastics is the most AI-ready company in Oklahoma** — a beyond-CWP discovery in Duncan that explicitly promotes scientific molding, decoupled processing, cavity pressure monitoring, gate freeze studies, and Design of Experiments on its website. Despite its small size, this is precisely the "data-rich, insight-poor" profile where industrial AI delivers immediate value.

**Da/Pro Rubber is a sophisticated multi-process molder** — LSR and plastic injection molding with micro-molding, two-shot capability, Class 10,000 cleanroom, ISO 9001, and global operations (Broken Arrow + MA + Singapore + Malaysia). The dual rubber/plastics capability and medical device focus create AI-ready process complexity.

The most striking finding is the **near-total absence of advanced process monitoring** across Oklahoma. No company has confirmed RJG eDart or CoPilot installations. No RJG Master Molders were found. Scientific molding adoption is minimal. This technology desert means the Alliance needs an evangelism approach rather than the "unlock existing data" entry point that works in more advanced markets. Family-ownership dominance (Gavco/Gavlik, ACO/3rd generation, KT/Townsend, Royal Vista, Ramtec) means decision-makers are accessible and relationship-driven — an advantage that partially offsets the 1,300-mile distance from Erie.

Three M&A transactions are relevant: PMC Global's acquisition of Discovery Plastics (April 2021), Franklin Electric's acquisition of Phil-Good Products (March 2023), and Georg Fischer's acquisition of Central Plastics (2008). Despite the national plastics M&A boom, no major PE platforms have consolidated Oklahoma injection molders.

**Yield: 3 HP, 4 MH, 5 Q, 2 Ref, 7 WL** (plus 6 excluded)

## QUESTIONS FOR BRETT

1. **Scientific molding evangelism vs. data-unlock:** Oklahoma has virtually zero RJG or cavity pressure monitoring. Does the Alliance have an engagement model for companies that need process monitoring fundamentals before AI optimization? True Position Plastics and Gavco could be test cases.
2. **Da/Pro Rubber scope question:** Da/Pro does both LSR injection molding and thermoplastic injection molding. Their Oklahoma facility may focus on rubber/LSR while Massachusetts handles thermoplastics. Is a dual-material molder with global operations and medical focus worth pursuing even if the OK facility is rubber-primary?
3. **Oil & gas specialty polymers:** Several Oklahoma companies (Advanced EMC Technologies, KT Plastics, Oil States) process difficult-to-mold materials (PEEK, PTFE, PFA) for oilfield applications. Do high-performance polymer processing challenges represent a differentiated AI value proposition vs. commodity PE/PP molding?
4. **NORDAM Group aerospace composites:** NORDAM does no injection molding but has a COO-level CWP contact and processes thermoplastic transparencies for Boeing/Airbus. Worth a Brett-level introduction for aerospace composites intelligence even though it's outside injection molding scope?

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### Oklahoma's technology gap is the opportunity
The near-total absence of RJG, cavity pressure monitoring, and scientific molding across Oklahoma injection molding means the Alliance cannot rely on the standard "unlock existing process data" approach. Instead, Oklahoma requires an evangelism model — helping companies adopt process monitoring fundamentals before advancing to AI-driven optimization. Gavco Plastics (existing Moldflow + engineering culture) and True Position Plastics (scientific molding DNA) are the natural beachheads.

### Family ownership dominance enables relationship-based selling
Nearly every independent Oklahoma molder is family-owned: Gavco (Gavlik), ACO (3rd generation), KT (Townsend), Royal Vista (Lawson), KECO (Keister → Kelley), Discovery (Paul → PE), Phil-Good (Phillips → Franklin Electric). Decision-makers are accessible without corporate procurement gates. This relationship-driven market favors Brett Hyder's consultative approach.

### The Tulsa–Broken Arrow cluster is the strategic concentration
Five of the state's most significant injection molding operations cluster in the Tulsa metro: Gavco Plastics, Da/Pro Rubber, RL Hudson, Royal Vista Plastics, and Advanced EMC Technologies. A single trip to Tulsa could cover the state's best prospects.

### Oklahoma–Arkansas–Missouri cross-border dynamics
Discovery Plastics in Miami, OK draws workforce from Joplin, MO (417 area codes among employees). Oklahoma Plastic Enterprises' management came from "large custom molders in Arkansas and Oklahoma." FM Corporation (Rogers, AR) and Johnson Manufacturing (Harrison, AR) appear in Oklahoma plastics directories. The eastern Oklahoma border is more connected to the AR/MO manufacturing ecosystem than to the TX corridor.

### Kenco Plastics closure (Poteau, 2016) left a gap
The 2016 shutdown of Kenco Plastics (~200 employees) in Poteau eliminated the only injection molder in eastern Oklahoma. Former Kenco employees may have dispersed to remaining OK molders or to Fort Smith, AR area operations. The blank CWP entry for Poteau is almost certainly a Kenco ghost record.

### No PE consolidation platform has entered Oklahoma
Despite the national plastics M&A boom ($9.4B in Q1 2025 alone), no major PE platforms have consolidated Oklahoma injection molders. This fragmented landscape of family-owned shops could be ripe for PE consolidation, making early Alliance relationships potentially valuable to future PE acquirers seeking data-ready operations.
