## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

1. **Sanford, ME is a micro-cluster.** Two of four confirmed injection molders (Casco Bay Molding and GVS North America) plus on-site toolmaker Legu Tool & Mold are all in Sanford (pop. ~22,000). G&G Products (Kennebunk) is 15 miles south. Three of four ME injection molders are within a 20-mile radius in York County.

2. **Zero RJG statewide.** No Maine injection molder shows any evidence of RJG, scientific molding software, Moldflow, or cavity pressure monitoring. The entire state represents an untapped market for data-driven process control — but at Tier 3 distance, only Casco Bay Molding has sufficient signals to warrant pursuing this gap.

3. **No IATF 16949 or AS9100 in Maine.** Despite proximity to Bath Iron Works (General Dynamics, ~6,800 employees building Navy destroyers), no Maine plastics company holds defense or automotive quality certifications. The defense supply chain in ME is metals-dominated.

4. **New England injection molding consolidation continues.** Form Tech Mold's 2020 acquisition by G&F Precision Molding (Sturbridge, MA) follows the same consolidation pattern seen in Erie's mold shop ecosystem. Independent ME tool shops are acquisition targets for larger New England molders seeking captive tooling.

5. **IDEXX as unserved local demand.** IDEXX's documented practice of sourcing injection molding from Connecticut while headquartered 30 miles from Casco Bay Molding highlights potential local supply chain optimization. If Casco Bay doesn't already serve IDEXX, this is a business development lead independent of the alliance.

6. **Nypro alumni network as engagement pathway.** Andrew Powell's Nypro background connects him to the largest alumni network in scientific injection molding. Brett may have Nypro contacts who could facilitate introduction. Clyde Magaw (deceased CWP contact) was also a 30-year Nypro veteran.
