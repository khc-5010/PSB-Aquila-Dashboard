## Executive Summary

Iowa's plastics manufacturing ecosystem is significantly deeper and more technologically sophisticated than its 63-row CWP footprint suggests, producing **6 High Priority, 11 Qualified, and 6 Watch List** prospects. The CWP dataset (63 rows, ~35 unique companies after dedup) had a **~46% exclusion rate** driven by John Deere (4 rows), Procter & Gamble (6 rows), Schneider Electric (3 rows), blow molders (Custom-Pak 3 rows, Agri-Industrial Plastics 3 rows), and rotational molders (Centro). The beyond-CWP discovery sweep was the critical differentiator, uncovering **i2-Tech** — a $48M, 250+ employee custom injection molder in West Des Moines ranked #137 on Plastics News' injection molders list — entirely absent from the CWP database.

**Critical CWP correction:** The hand-off prompt assumed Dickten Masch Plastics (Ankeny) was acquired by Nypro → Jabil. **This is wrong.** The actual chain was Dickten Masch → Everett Smith Group (2004) → Techniplas (2010) → Chapter 11 (May 2020) → **Revere Plastics Systems** acquired the Ankeny plant for ~$2.13M (July 2020). Revere is PE-backed by French firm Ardian and is a top-100 North American molder. This is an active, legitimate prospect that was nearly excluded by a faulty assumption.

**#1 Iowa prospect: Accumold (Ankeny, IA)** — world's leading micro-injection molder, ~350 employees, ~175 presses (half proprietary Micro-Mold® machines), ISO 13485/9001/14001, cleanrooms, $50–92M revenue, family/founder-owned, already deploying AI for quoting and historical data analysis. The AI adoption cuts both ways — it confirms extreme readiness but may reduce need for Alliance services.

**Key Iowa patterns:**
- **AI adoption already underway at the top:** Accumold publicly announced AI integration in March 2025. This is the first company in the entire Tier 3 pipeline with confirmed, active AI deployment. Alliance positioning must emphasize capabilities beyond what Accumold has implemented in-house.
- **IQMS/DELMIAworks is the leading ERP signal:** Lomont Molding runs IQMS across all facilities — the strongest ERP-readiness indicator in Iowa. No RJG installations were confirmed among any Iowa independent.
- **EPC's 21-plant patchwork is the highest-value corporate target:** The Grinnell HQ with President Reza Kargarzadeh + 3 acquisitions in November 2025 alone = massive legacy-data integration challenge. This is precisely the problem the Alliance solves.
- **Blow molding exclusion rate is high:** Agri-Industrial Plastics (200 employees, 28 machines), Custom-Pak (500–1,000 employees, 16 locations), Herman Mold + Tooling (blow mold maker), and Warren Distribution are all blow molding operations. Iowa's agricultural economy drives blow molding demand for fuel tanks, tanks, and containers.
- **Zero confirmed RJG installations among Iowa independents** — a greenfield opportunity matching the Kentucky pattern.

**Geographic clusters for outreach:**
- **Des Moines metro (Ankeny/West Des Moines/Huxley/Cumming):** Accumold + i2-Tech + Revere Plastics Ankeny + Kreg Tool (captive). Four significant operations within 30 miles, including the #1 and #3 Iowa prospects.
- **Cedar Rapids/Eastern Iowa corridor:** MSI Mold Builders + BeraTek + Geometric Mold + Quantum Plastics (Victor). Mold-making hub.
- **Southeast Iowa (Fairfield/Mt Pleasant/Bloomfield):** Lomont Molding + HCM Plastics + Angstrom Precision + Herman Mold. Manufacturing heritage belt with injection + blow + mold making.
- **Grinnell (standalone):** EPC corporate HQ — warrants dedicated corporate-level engagement.
