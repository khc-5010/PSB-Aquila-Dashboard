## Executive Summary

**Utah pipeline: 5 High Priority | 8 Qualified | 10 Watch List | 4 Excluded**

Utah's CWP database is dominated by medical device OEM captive operations — Becton Dickinson alone accounts for 9 of 41 rows (22%), and Fresenius Medical Care adds 3 more. After excluding OEM captives and non-targets, the CWP yields only ~8-10 independent companies to investigate. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep proved essential, surfacing Utah's single most important prospect and several major operations invisible to the CWP.

**Headline finding:** Rosti Utah (Clearfield) is the single highest-priority prospect identified in the entire Utah ecosystem and potentially one of the strongest in the national pipeline. The facility has invested **$1.2 million in RJG eDart systems deployed on all 30+ presses**, and its Director of Engineering has publicly stated a desire to combine AI and machine learning with RJG data for real-time process control. This is a textbook Aquila readiness signal — structured cavity-pressure data at scale with no AI layer, plus explicit stated demand.

**Key corrections:** ANVS Inc. is a night vision equipment distributor (not a molder). Wolfe Tory Medical is a device assembly operation (no injection molding). Alpha Packaging SLC is blow molding only. Sun Products Corp is now Henkel AG — a €20B+ German CPG captive.

**Ecosystem character:** ~25-30 injection molding operations along the Wasatch Front corridor (Ogden–SLC–Provo), heavily skewed toward medical/life sciences. PE and corporate M&A activity is intense — 6 of the 8 largest molding operations have PE or corporate parent backing. No IATF 16949 automotive molders were found anywhere in the state.

## 1. HIGH PRIORITY (5 Companies)

### 1. Rosti Utah ★★★★★
**Formerly:** SMI Molding (1981) → Plastic Components Inc. (PCI) | **Parent:** Rosti Group (Sweden) | **Location:** Clearfield, UT

**Beyond-CWP discovery.** This is the single most AI-ready injection molder identified in Utah and one of the strongest prospects in the entire national pipeline.

**Profile:** 62,000 sq ft facility, 30+ injection molding cells (55–610 tons), 75+ employees, ISO 9001:2015 certified. Markets include medical, sporting goods, home fashions, consumer, industrial, and electronics, processing 100+ different resin types. Capabilities span elastomers, TPEs, urethanes, gas-assist, over-molding, insert molding, 2K/multi-shot, rapid prototyping, and reshoring/tooling transfer. Currently building a new ISO Class 8 cleanroom with 5 molding presses for medical device production.

**RJG deployment (the critical signal):** Beginning September 2009 with two eDart systems, PCI executed a systematic two-year initiative to equip every press. By 2011, all new presses were spec'd with eDart systems hardwired at delivery. Total investment exceeds **$1.2 million** in RJG technology. Every mold is designed for in-mold pressure sensing. RJG process mapping is mandatory for all new mold qualifications. Shot-to-shot history is saved in eDart for troubleshooting. An RJG trainer visits quarterly for refresher training and operator audits. Staff includes **2 Master Molders and 3 Master Molder Level I**.

**The AI demand signal:** Director of Engineering Kurt Behrendt stated publicly: *"We're interested in combining AI and machine learning with RJG to improve our real-time process control… we want to take it to the next level by being proactive in reducing the variables altogether. This effort will require advanced capabilities to analyze the RJG outputs and create action plans."*

At the corporate level, Rosti Group CEO Jonas Persson confirmed in November 2025 that Rosti is investing heavily in automation and AI-driven systems, with Technology & Innovation Director Scott Dickson targeting an internal AI proof-of-concept by end of 2025 and large-scale testing at one site before global rollout.

**M&A lineage:** Sage Park acquired SMI Molding (2017) → Plastic Components Inc./MPE Partners acquired SMI (August 2021) → Rosti Group (Sweden) acquired PCI (January 2022), gaining its first US footprint across Wisconsin, North Carolina, and Utah.

**Alliance fit:** Textbook. Full RJG eDart across all presses = rich structured process data on every cycle. Explicit stated demand for AI/ML integration. Scientific molding culture with Master Molders. Swedish parent pursuing AI strategy. New cleanroom expansion creates immediate opportunity. **Recommend immediate outreach.**

**Key contacts:** Kurt Behrendt (Director of Engineering), Lukasz Sekula (Managing Director).

**Cross-tier note:** Rosti also has a facility in Searcy, AR (1 CWP contact: Alan Freeland, Engineering Manager) — relevant for the upcoming Arkansas Tier 3 analysis.

### 2. Biomerics ★★★★
**Formerly:** Utah Plastics Group Inc. (1994) | **Parent:** ONCAP / Wasatch Equity Partners (PE) | **Location:** Salt Lake City, UT (HQ) + 9 US facilities

**Beyond-CWP discovery.** Critical finding: "Utah Plastics Group Inc." is the former name of Biomerics, founded in 1994 by Jeff Clark with 3 employees and 1 customer.

**Profile:** Vertically integrated medical device contract manufacturer (CDMO) with ~$800M revenue and 1,500–3,200 employees. Additional capabilities span polymer compounding, extrusion, laser processing, catheter manufacturing, and finished device assembly. 220,000 sq ft of ISO Class 8 cleanroom environments. ISO 13485:2016 certified. Serves 20 of the top 30 medical device OEMs globally.

**M&A history:** Serial acquirer. Dependable Plastics (2022, created IMP division), Precision Concepts Medical (2023, ONCAP became largest shareholder). New CEO Arun Mohan appointed October 2025.

**Alliance fit:** Very high. 100+ presses generating massive process data. Active M&A means constant facility integration challenges. ISO 13485 creates rich data trails. However, scale may mean longer sales cycles and corporate procurement gatekeeping.

### 3. EnviroTech Molded Products ★★★★
**Parent:** Klinkau Group (Germany) | **Location:** Salt Lake City, UT | **CWP Contacts:** 2

The strongest custom molder in the CWP list. Specializes in the largest injected polymer products on the market — presses from 850 to 2,500 tons with single-shot capacity up to 530 pounds and wall thicknesses exceeding 5 inches. Revenue ~$22.8M, 47–130 employees.

**Critical technology signal:** Proprietary "Bulk Injection Molding" process using custom-designed accumulators and a two-stage injection system with company-built process controllers running proprietary software. Also runs four JSW machines modified for heavy-wall pump components. Industries include oil & gas, chemical processing, mining, semiconductors, defense (Air Force), and marine. Materials: PVDF, Kynar, Ultem, PPS, fluoropolymers.

**Alliance fit:** High. Proprietary process controllers with custom software represent exactly the kind of legacy process data the alliance targets. Complex thick-wall molding with engineering resins generates significant quality challenges.

**CWP contacts:** Damir Olujic (Process Engineer), Jon Roesler (Mold Engineering Manager).
