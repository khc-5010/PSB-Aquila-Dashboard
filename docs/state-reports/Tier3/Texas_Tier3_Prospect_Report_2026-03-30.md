# ALLIANCE PROSPECT REPORT — Tier 3 Texas
## March 30, 2026

**Search Focus:** Converters and Mold/Tool Makers in Texas — Gulf Coast, DFW, Rio Grande Valley, and West Texas clusters
**Source:** Michelle Hartmann's CWP database (9,750 rows → 164 TX plastics-type rows → 123 unique companies after dedup → 30 researched)
**Companies Profiled (Deep Research):** 30 total — 7 High Priority, 9 Qualified, 5 Watch List, 9 Not Target (removed/reclassified)
**CWP Cross-References Found:** 1 confirmed PSB/Penn State connection (Ring Container Technologies — lead donor)

**Tier 3 threshold applied:** Texas companies required multiple strong readiness signals to justify geographic distance from Erie, PA. Single-signal companies were deprioritized to Qualified or Watch List regardless of category fit. The Houston petrochemical corridor created heavy noise — 42% of all TX plastics-type companies were excluded as resin suppliers, OEMs, or non-targets at Pass 1.

## NOTES

### Texas Market Observations
- **Houston petrochemical noise is extreme.** 42% of all CWP plastics-type companies in Texas were resin suppliers, petrochemical companies, or non-plastics OEMs — the highest exclusion rate of any state processed. The Houston corridor is fundamentally a raw materials market, not a converting market. Converters exist in Houston but are outnumbered by their suppliers.

- **PE saturation in Texas plastics.** Five of the seven High Priority prospects are PE-backed (Tide Rock, Platinum Equity, BDT & MSD Partners, Wynnchurch Capital, SCF Partners). This is a higher PE concentration than any other state in the pipeline. Combined, these five companies represent ~$700M–$1.3B+ in revenue. PE firms drive operational improvement and digital transformation mandates — natural alignment with Alliance offerings.

- **Rio Grande Valley reality check.** The McAllen/Mission/Brownsville cluster has plastics operations but they're predominantly small-scale, maquiladora-adjacent, and serving cross-border supply chains. Alpha XL, Hi-Tech (now Royal Technologies), EMU Plastics, and King's ECO are the confirmed manufacturers. Most lack the signal density for Tier 3 prioritization. The exception is King's ECO with its automotive certification and 1,000T press capacity.

- **Sweetwater cluster resolved.** Not a cluster — a single corporate family (Ludlum Measurements). All 6 CWP contacts across Ludlum, ADIT PMT, and West Texas Molding should be treated as one account.

- **4OEM Plastics LLC (San Antonio) — new prospect.** Former Schlemmer USA executives founded this company in 2020 to serve the North American automotive wire harness protection market with injection molded corrugated tubing. Not in the CWP database but warrants investigation as a prospect.

### Cross-Pipeline Flags
- **Royal Technologies (Hi-Tech Plastics parent)** — HQ in Hudsonville, MI. Should be routed to the Tier 2 Michigan workstream rather than engaged as a Texas prospect. $283M revenue, 315+ presses, ESOP-owned. Significant target.
- **Ring Container Technologies** — Has a Hanover, PA plant. The PSB donor relationship could be leveraged through that facility for closer engagement.
- **DuraLine** — PA Turnpike project (500+ miles of MicroDucts) is a PA connection point even though the company is TX-headquartered.
