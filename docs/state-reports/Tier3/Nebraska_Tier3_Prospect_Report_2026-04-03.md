## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Nebraska's injection molding landscape is structurally captive-heavy, with BD, Molex, and Schneider Electric collectively operating ~580+ presses in-house. The independent custom molder segment is thinner than peer Midwest states and has contracted with two recent closures (Denver Plastics Nebraska 2022, Five Star Molding 2023). This scarcity elevates the value of surviving independents.

**Majors Plastics is the clear #1 target** — a 156-press ESOP with ISO 14644 Class 7 cleanroom, blue-chip customers (3M Supplier of the Year 2x, BD Supply Chain Excellence), and no confirmed advanced process monitoring. HTI Plastics is the strongest CWP-connected target with confirmed RJG Master Molders and Moldflow usage. The CWP record "Lenco Inc.-PMC" is stale data — the Waverly facility was acquired by Commercial Plastics Co. in 2015 and already runs RJG eDart. Design Plastics' acquisition by Coda Resources in January 2024 creates a post-acquisition technology investment window.

The Grand Island/Hastings/Kearney corridor yielded fewer independents than expected — Midland Molding is essentially the sole surviving custom molder in central Nebraska after Five Star Molding dissolved. The agricultural economy supports steady but not growing demand for durable molded components; real growth signals are in medical (BD's $200M+ investment, HTI's pharma focus) and automotive (Triad Fastener's IATF certification, Vantec's Angstrom acquisition).

**Yield: 4 HP, 5 Q, 6 WL** (plus 5 captive, 3 excluded/removed, 2 closed)

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### Patterns observed
1. **Nebraska's independent molder market is contracting.** Two closures in 18 months (Denver Plastics 2022, Five Star 2023) have concentrated demand among fewer survivors. This makes surviving independents busier and potentially more receptive to productivity-enhancing technology.
2. **The captive-to-independent ratio is extreme.** ~580+ captive presses (BD, Molex, Schneider) vs. ~290+ independent presses (Majors 156, Vantec 49, HTI 31, Commercial Plastics 60 site, Dimatic 20, Triad 19, etc.). Nebraska's plastics economy is overwhelmingly captive OEM.
3. **Central NE corridor is a mold-builder hub, not a molder hub.** Dramco Tool (Grand Island, 65 employees) and DY-NA Tool (Kearney) are significant tooling operations that serve molders regionally. Midland Molding is the only surviving independent molder in the corridor.
4. **Medical is the growth vertical.** BD's $200M+ investment, HTI's 50% pharma/medical revenue, embecta's insulin syringe dominance, and Commercial Plastics' FDA compliance all point to medical as the strongest end-market signal.
5. **Central Community College (Columbus) offers RJG training programs** — companies sending employees to these programs are pre-qualified technology-adoption prospects. Worth monitoring enrollee companies.

### Questions for Brett Hyder
1. **Majors Plastics' 156-press ESOP with no confirmed RJG:** Is this the highest-press-count independent molder in the pipeline without cavity pressure monitoring? If so, it may represent the single largest process monitoring opportunity by machine count.
2. **Commercial Plastics' multi-site RJG deployment:** They already have RJG eDart across their platform. Is the AI-interpretation-of-existing-RJG-data pitch the right approach, or would process standardization across 5 acquired plants (each with different legacy systems) be a stronger entry?
