## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### North Dakota has fewer than 400 injection molding employees statewide
The comprehensive web discovery sweep confirmed that North Dakota's entire injection molding workforce totals fewer than 400 people across 2–3 active operations (ComDel ~300+, Terhorst ~30–80, GPPM ~11–50 if active). For comparison, a single mid-size injection molder in Pennsylvania or Minnesota often employs 300+ people. This is the thinnest injection molding state in the pipeline by a significant margin.

### The Fargo-Moorhead corridor has NO custom injection molders
Despite being ND's largest metro area (~260,000), Fargo-Moorhead has zero confirmed custom injection molding operations. Dakota Molding (rotational), Tecton (pultrusion), Horn Plastics (distribution), and Cope Plastics (distribution) are present but use different processes. Great Plains Plastic Molding's status is uncertain. This is a notable gap given Bobcat/Doosan's West Fargo headquarters and the demand for injection-molded components.

### Bobcat's plastic parts supply chain is overwhelmingly out-of-state
Terhorst Manufacturing (Minot, 250+ miles from West Fargo) is the only confirmed ND-based injection molding supplier to Bobcat. Panjiva trade data shows Bobcat imports plastics from China. The vast majority of Bobcat's injection-molded panels, shrouds, housings, seats, guards, and handles appear to be sourced from Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, or international suppliers. This represents a significant local supply chain gap.

### ComDel Innovation is an outlier — sophisticated operation in an unexpected location
ComDel's combination of 300+ employees, ESOP ownership, ISO 13485/9001/14001 certifications, FDA registration, cleanroom expansion, in-house tooling, simulation analysis, ROCTOOL technology, and materials characterization lab would be notable in any state. Finding it in Wahpeton, ND (population ~8,000) is genuinely surprising. The Imation facility inheritance (25-acre campus) provided the physical infrastructure; the founders' 3M/Imation pedigree provided the operational sophistication.

### NDSU's Coatings & Polymeric Materials program is world-class but coatings-focused
NDSU's CPM Department (est. 1906) is the only formal coatings and polymeric materials department in any U.S. university. Focus is polymer chemistry, surface science, and coatings formulation — not injection molding process manufacturing. Some injection-molding-relevant coursework exists in ME (ME 435: Plastics and Polymer Processing in Manufacturing). NDSU has an SPE Student Chapter. ComDel actively partners with NDSU for internships. No PSB/Behrend connections identified.

### Technology desert — zero RJG, scientific molding, or process monitoring signals statewide
No North Dakota company shows any evidence of RJG, cavity pressure monitoring, scientific molding methodology, decoupled molding, or modern ERP/MES systems. ComDel's injection molding simulation (thermal/stress) is the closest signal to data-oriented process engineering, but it's design-phase simulation rather than production-phase monitoring. This is the widest technology gap of any Tier 3 state surveyed — broader than Montana or Wyoming.
