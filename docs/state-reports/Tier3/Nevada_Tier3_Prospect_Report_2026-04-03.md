## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Nevada is the thinnest injection molding market analyzed in the Tier 3 sweep. The CWP database was a complete wash — all six entries are either excluded entities (Berry Global), confirmed extruders (Primex Plastics), non-manufacturers (PEP Innovations, smlm llc), equipment dealers (A.C. Wilson), or Mexico-based manufacturers with NV administrative addresses (NMH Marketing). The beyond-CWP sweep did all the heavy lifting and surfaced approximately 12 injection molding operations statewide, concentrated in two corridors: Reno/Sparks/Carson City (7–8 companies) and Las Vegas metro (4–5 companies including two major new entrants).

Three companies qualify as High Priority despite maximum geographic distance from PSB (~2,300+ miles): Triad Plastic Technologies (Reno — ISO 13485 cleanroom molder with MoldFlow and in-house tooling), Cavist Corporation (Reno — world's largest electronics overmolding facility, ESOP since 2023), and Messenger Molding (Reno — precision specialist, family-owned, CA transplant since 1994). All three are in the Reno corridor.

The most striking finding: **no Nevada company was found using RJG, cavity pressure monitoring, or formal scientific molding practices.** This represents a significant technology adoption gap and a clear Alliance value proposition — the Alliance would not be competing against existing process monitoring vendors but introducing entirely new capability.

Two major Las Vegas investments (Kreate = $11.5M/235K sqft operational; UPM = potential $32M/100K sqft relocation from CA) could double southern Nevada's injection molding capacity, but both are commodity/general purpose operations rather than precision/technical molders.

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

**Nevada is an RJG/cavity pressure technology desert.** No company in the state was found using RJG eDart systems, cavity pressure monitoring, or formal scientific molding practices. MoldFlow was mentioned only by Triad. No company referenced ERP/MES systems or Industry 4.0 by name. This represents both a market reality (thin manufacturing base = thin technology vendor coverage) and a significant Alliance opportunity (no competition from existing process monitoring vendors).

**The Reno corridor is the only viable engagement cluster.** Three HP targets (Triad, Cavist, Messenger Molding) plus two Q targets (Precision Molding & Machining, Carson Hi-Tech) are all within a 30-mile radius of Reno. A single site visit could address all five prospects. The Las Vegas metro targets (Kreate, Impact International, future UPM) are 450 miles south and lower priority.

**ESOP ownership is a recurring pattern in NV molders.** Cavist (ESOP since 2023) and the broader trend of employee-owned manufacturers in the region suggest cultural receptivity to investment in workforce-empowering technology like AI process tools.

**California-to-Nevada manufacturing migration is real but has not yet produced a major injection molding influx.** Messenger Molding (1994) and potentially UPM (2024–2026) represent the only confirmed injection molder relocations. The trend is more pronounced in logistics, distribution, and light assembly. However, NV's tax advantages and growing manufacturing infrastructure (Tesla/Panasonic ecosystem, GOED incentive programs) suggest more may follow.

**Carson City is a hidden mold-making cluster.** Two dedicated mold shops (High Desert Tool & Mold, Mattox Machine & Molding) plus two injection molders (Carson Hi-Tech, Impact International) operate within Carson City — a disproportionate concentration for a city of ~58,000. This likely reflects proximity to Reno manufacturing demand plus favorable Carson City business conditions.

**Berry Global Henderson facility may be at risk.** Berry's 15–20 plant closure rationalization (2023–2025) and the Amcor merger create uncertainty about the Henderson plant's long-term status. This is intelligence for the active alliance relationship, not for prospecting.
