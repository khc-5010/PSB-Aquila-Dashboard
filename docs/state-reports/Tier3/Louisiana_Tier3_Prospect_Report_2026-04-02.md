# ALLIANCE PROSPECT REPORT — Louisiana (Tier 3)
# Date: April 2, 2026

**Search Focus:** Converters and Mold/Tool Makers in Louisiana
**Companies Surfaced:** 22 total — 10 converters/molders, 2 integrated mold maker+molders, 10 captive/excluded/other
**CWP Database Entries:** 31 rows → ~16 unique companies after deduplication (13 rows = Intralox name variants)
**Beyond-CWP Discoveries:** 3 confirmed new companies (KRC Custom Manufacturing, Raytech Industries, Lotel)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Louisiana's injection molding landscape is the thinnest of any Tier 3 state analyzed — approximately 12–15 active operations statewide, overwhelmed by the state's dominant petrochemical/resin production base. The CWP database is almost useless for finding converters here: 13 of 31 rows are Intralox name variants, and most remaining entries are resin producers, trading companies, or non-manufacturers. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep was therefore the primary pipeline source, confirming the pattern seen in Washington (Piller Aimmco), Georgia (Accurate Plastics & Mold), and other states where the database misses key targets.

**Key findings:**
- **4 High Priority** prospects with multiple strong readiness signals
- **5 Qualified** prospects worth monitoring or engaging
- **5 Watch List** companies with limited signals but worth tracking
- **No PE acquisitions** of Louisiana injection molders identified in the 2023–2025 window. All confirmed molders are independently or family-owned.
- **No AS9100-certified** injection molders in the state despite significant aerospace/defense presence (NASA Michoud, Barksdale AFB)
- **Zero PEEK injection molding capability** identified — the high-performance oilfield polymer niche that should theoretically exist in Louisiana simply doesn't. Offshore energy components requiring specialty polymers are molded in Texas.
- **Top prospect:** Noble Plastics (Grand Coteau) — RJG eDart on every machine, IQMS ERP, ITAR/CMMC compliant, $8.5M expansion, scientific molding culture, family-owned. The strongest single-company AI readiness profile in any Tier 3 state.
- **Largest operation:** Intralox/Laitram (Harahan/Hammond) — 160+ injection molding presses, 29+ person in-house mold shop, Oracle ERP, $1B+ parent revenue. 100% captive but generates more process data than most entire state pipelines combined.
- **Lafayette/Acadiana cluster** is real: Noble Plastics, ASH Industries, Acadiana Plastics Molding, and Advanced Plastics Mfg. all operate within 25 miles of Lafayette.

### The oilfield injection molding gap
The hand-off prompt hypothesized that Louisiana's oil & gas industry would support a distinctive custom molding niche for offshore energy components. **This hypothesis was not supported by research.** No Louisiana injection molder advertises API certification, PEEK capability, or specialized oilfield component manufacturing. Noble Plastics serves O&G customers with corrosion-resistant and flame-resistant thermoplastics, but this is a secondary market, not a primary focus. The high-performance polymer oilfield molding niche is dominated by Texas operations. Louisiana's oil industry creates demand for valves (Kerotest serves gas distribution, not oilfield) and pipeline components, but these are primarily metal or extruded PE, not injection molded.
