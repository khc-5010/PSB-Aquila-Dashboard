## Executive Summary

Alabama's plastics manufacturing ecosystem is significantly deeper than its 52-row CWP footprint suggests, producing **6 High Priority, 9 Qualified, and 10 Watch List** prospects. The CWP dataset (52 rows, ~30 unique companies after dedup) had a **~50% exclusion rate** driven by Honda (3 rows), Parker Hannifin (2 rows), resin suppliers (Asahi Kasei, Indorama), and textile manufacturers (Twitchell, Phifer). The beyond-CWP discovery sweep was the primary pipeline source, uncovering 20+ companies absent from the database — including **Engineered Plastic Components** ($275M, two AL plants, the single largest CWP omission in the entire Tier 3 series).

**Critical CWP data quality finding:** Am Pro Custom Molding (Anniston) was **acquired by IAC Group in November 2010** and now operates as a 325-employee captive automotive plant. The CWP contact (President E. Burns Roensch) departed 15+ years ago. This is the most significant stale-data ghost discovered in any Tier 3 state.

**#1 Alabama prospect: Innovative Plastics (Madison, AL)** — 20 injection presses, $13.9M revenue, ISO 9001:2015, in-house toolroom with mold design-through-production capability, 3D prototyping, insert molding, sonic welding. Alabama Manufacturer of the Year. Huntsville corridor location. Family-accessible through parent T-H Marine.

**Key Alabama patterns:**
- **RJG presence confirmed but limited:** Prudent American Technologies (Huntsville) advertises RJG eDART + Moldex3D — the only company in AL doing so. RJG Inc. operates a training center at Southern Union State Community College in Opelika, confirming regional injection molding density.
- **Auburn/Opelika is a stealth plastics cluster:** CSP Technologies/Aptar ($555M), SiO2 Medical (post-bankruptcy/Oaktree), I-PEX USA (precision micro-molding), Revere Plastics (gas-assist/two-shot), Cumberland Plastic Solutions (33–43 machines), and the RJG training hub — all within 30 miles.
- **Foreign-owned captive density is high:** Kumi/Kamco (Japan/Honda), Moriroku/Rainsville Technology (Japan/Honda), Plasman (Canada/PE), MöllerTech (Germany/Mercedes), SCA (Korea/Hyundai-Kia), IAC/Am Pro (global Tier I). Most unlikely to buy AI independently of parent mandates.
- **SyBridge Technologies Alabama (Cullman)** is the strongest mold-making discovery — 43–45 employees, formerly Concours Mold, serves Tesla/GM/Ford/BMW, tryout presses to 3,300 tons.

**Geographic clusters for outreach:**
- **Auburn/Opelika cluster:** Cumberland Plastic Solutions + Revere Plastics + I-PEX USA + (contextually) CSP/Aptar. Dense independent molder concentration with RJG training infrastructure nearby.
- **Huntsville/Madison corridor:** Innovative Plastics + American Precision Products + Prudent American Technologies. Defense-certified molders in fastest-growing AL metro.
- **Florence/Muscle Shoals:** Steff Industries + E.S. Robbins + PROJECTXYZ + SyBridge/Concours (Cullman, 45 min east). Manufacturing heritage belt.
- **Birmingham I-65 spine:** EPC Bessemer + EPC Leeds + Koller Craft South (Gadsden). Automotive supply chain corridor.

## Alabama Market Intelligence Notes

**RJG presence is confirmed but sparse.** Prudent American Technologies (Huntsville) is the only company advertising RJG eDART. The RJG training center at Southern Union State Community College (Opelika) was established in partnership with RJG Inc. — attendee/graduate lists would be a high-value lead source for identifying companies investing in scientific molding training.

**Auburn/Opelika is Alabama's hidden plastics hub.** Six significant injection molding operations cluster within 30 miles: CSP Technologies/Aptar, I-PEX USA, Revere Plastics, Cumberland Plastic Solutions, SCA Inc., and (nearby) SiO2 Medical.
