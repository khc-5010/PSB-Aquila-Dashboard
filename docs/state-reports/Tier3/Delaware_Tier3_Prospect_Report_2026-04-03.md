## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Delaware's manufacturing base is dominated by financial services (Wilmington) and poultry processing (Delmarva Peninsula), with very limited injection molding activity. However, the two confirmed prospects — Craig Technologies and Atlantis Industries — are both excellent fits with dual injection molding and in-house tooling capabilities, multiple certifications, and decades of legacy data. Craig Technologies in particular was the strongest single CWP signal in the entire final batch, with a dedicated "Molding Manager" contact.

Key findings:
- **Craig Technologies** (Seaford) — Strongest CWP lead. Multi-division plastics campus: injection molding + in-house tooling division (formerly Linus Tooling) + clean rooms + compression molding. ISO 9001. Serial acquirer. High-performance materials (Torlon, PEEK).
- **Atlantis Industries** (Georgetown) — Deepest certification stack in DE. IATF 16949 + ITAR + CMMC 2.0 + Moldflow/FEA. In-house tooling. Purpose-built 40K sq ft facility (2018). Lean/Six Sigma with Black Belt.

The beyond-CWP sweep confirmed that Delaware has essentially no other injection molding operations beyond these two. Several NJ/PA companies serve DE customers but operate no DE facilities.

## NOTES

- Delaware's injection molding industry is genuinely thin — essentially just Craig Technologies and Atlantis Industries on the Delmarva Peninsula (Sussex County). Northern Delaware (Wilmington/Newark/New Castle) is financial services and chemical industry headquarters with no injection molding operations.
- Both HP companies are in Sussex County, southern Delaware — over 100 miles from Wilmington and closer to Salisbury, MD than to any DE metro area. This is a manufacturing corridor separate from DE's corporate/financial identity.
- The DuPont legacy in Wilmington created deep polymer science expertise (University of Delaware's polymer program is nationally recognized) but this expertise feeds academic research and material science, not injection molding production. No DuPont spin-off injection molding companies were identified in DE.
- No RJG or cavity pressure monitoring identified at any DE company. This represents an industry-wide gap.
- The beyond-CWP sweep was the least productive of any state — confirming that DE's injection molding sector is genuinely limited to the two CWP-identified companies. Multiple directory searches and geographic sweeps returned no additional confirmed injection molders operating in Delaware.
