## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kansas's injection molding landscape is thin but contains genuine high-value targets obscured by a CWP database heavily weighted toward captive operations and non-molders. Of 18 CWP companies investigated, only 3 are confirmed active custom or semi-custom injection molders; 5 are captive operations; 3 facilities have permanently closed since 2023; and the remaining 7 are not injection molders at all. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep proved critical, surfacing the state's two strongest targets (Wescon Plastics, GemTech LLC) plus two smaller custom molders (BPE Manufacturing, Wolgram Engineered Plastics).

**Key findings:**
- **3 High Priority** prospects with multiple strong readiness signals
- **5 Qualified** prospects worth monitoring or engaging
- **5 Watch List** captive operations (Viega, Rehrig Pacific, Coleman/Newell, CertainTeed/Saint-Gobain, Standard Motor Products)
- **3 CWP facilities permanently closed** since 2023: Silgan Dispensing/Winfield (Jan 2024, 169 layoffs), Winfield Consumer Products/Husky Liners (June 2023), Orbis Corporation/Osage City (March 2025)
- **Zero PE acquisitions** of Kansas injection molders in the 2023–2025 window. Wescon Plastics (Arcane Capital, Dec 2023) is the only PE-backed molder in the state.
- **Zero RJG-equipped** molders identified — first Tier 3 state with no RJG signal at all
- **Zero AS9100-certified** injection molders despite Wichita's aerospace concentration
- **Top prospect:** Wescon Plastics (Wichita) — scientific molding, PE-backed, $25–50M, PEEK/engineering resins, multi-industry. The "scientific molding without AI" archetype.
- **McPherson cluster confirmed:** Ferguson/Revere (36 presses), CertainTeed (captive siding), Viega (20 machines, captive fittings) — three injection molding operations in a town of 14,000.
- **KC metro dominates discovery:** GemTech, Wolgram, Churchill Container, and BPE all in the KC metro or nearby.
- **Pittsburg State University** operates one of the nation's leading Plastics Engineering Technology programs in southeast Kansas — potential academic ecosystem connection but also a PSB competitor for plastics workforce development.

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### The Winfield collapse is dramatic
Two separate manufacturing plants in the same small town (pop. ~12,000) closed within 18 months: Silgan Dispensing (Jan 2024, 169 jobs) and Husky Liners/RealTruck (June 2023). Combined ~200+ manufacturing jobs lost. This eliminates Winfield as a plastics cluster and creates a significant displaced workforce pool — companies hiring in the Wichita area (45 miles north) could draw from this labor.

### McPherson cluster is real but captive-dominated
Three injection molding operations in a town of 14,000: Ferguson/Revere (36 presses, custom — HP), CertainTeed (captive siding), Viega (20 machines, captive fittings). Only Ferguson/Revere is addressable. CertainTeed and Viega represent significant molding volume (Viega alone produces ~7M parts/month) but technology decisions flow through Paris and Germany respectively.

### Kansas has no RJG footprint
Zero Kansas molders showed RJG equipment, Master Molder certification, or cavity pressure monitoring of any kind. This is the first Tier 3 state with a complete absence of RJG signals. The closest RJG presence is Burger & Brown Engineering in Grandview, MO (just across the KC state line), which manufacturers the SMARTFLOW® mold temperature control line and has RJG Certified Master Molders on staff — a potential ecosystem referral but not a Kansas prospect.

### Pittsburg State University = competitive intelligence
Pittsburg State University in southeast Kansas operates a nationally recognized Plastics Engineering Technology program with injection molding equipment and industry partnerships. This is a potential PSB competitor for workforce development and industry engagement in the Kansas/Oklahoma/Missouri tri-state area. Companies in southeast Kansas (Charloma/Cherryvale) may have existing Pitt State relationships rather than PSB connections.

### Aerospace gap is striking
Wichita hosts Boeing, Textron Aviation (Cessna/Beechcraft), Spirit AeroSystems, Learjet, and Airbus operations — yet zero dedicated aerospace injection molding suppliers were identified in the state. No AS9100-certified injection molders exist in Kansas. Aerospace plastics for Wichita OEMs are sourced from out-of-state. The NIAR/ATLAS composite molding R&D center at Wichita State focuses on composite materials and high-speed automated placement, not conventional injection molding.

### FirmaPak / Container Services represents notable PE activity
Shore Capital Partners completed a partnership with Container Services (Hillsboro, KS) in 2023, creating FirmaPak as a blow molding growth platform with subsequent acquisitions. While blow molding (not injection), this validates Kansas plastics sector attractiveness to PE sponsors and could signal broader PE interest in the state's manufacturing base.
