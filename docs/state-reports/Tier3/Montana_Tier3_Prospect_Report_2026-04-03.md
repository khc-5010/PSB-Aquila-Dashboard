## QUESTIONS FOR BRETT

1. **PDM Plastics multi-facility data integration:** PDM operates injection molding in Manhattan, MT and Ciudad Juárez, Mexico, with tooling sourcing from Shenzhen, China. The 2021 ownership transition (founder Mike Groff stepped back; management buyout by Randy Scheid, James Smith, Darrell Witham) means new leadership is still integrating a three-continent operation. Is the cross-border data integration challenge a distinctive AI value proposition vs. domestic-only operations?

2. **Diversified Plastics' UHMW injection molding specialty:** DPI Missoula has a unique capability injection molding UHMW (ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene) — a material most molders can't run. Their clients describe it as "sets them apart from everyone worldwide." Does this specialty material processing create different AI optimization challenges than standard thermoplastic injection molding? Are Aquila's models trained on or adaptable to UHMW processing parameters?

3. **Captive OEM with proprietary material formulation:** West Paw (Bozeman) molds ~300 SKUs using proprietary Zogoflex and Seaflex recycled-ocean-plastic formulations. They've invested in automation/robotics and run two factories. Is a captive OEM with proprietary material formulations and growing automation an interesting edge case, or firmly outside scope as a branded product company?

4. **"Manhattan, MT plastics cluster" — smallest cluster in pipeline:** Manhattan (pop. ~2,000) has three plastics-related manufacturers: PDM (injection molding), Blackhawk/Revelyst (captive), and Montana Advanced Composites (prepregs/laminates). Mike Groff (PDM founder) called it "the plastic mecca of Montana." Is this micro-cluster worth a single-trip engagement concept, or is the geographic distance too extreme to justify?

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

**Montana's injection molding ecosystem is thin but genuine.** The state has approximately 6–8 injection molding operations total (PDM, DPI Missoula, Northern Plastics, West Paw, Steel Reality, Blackhawk/Revelyst, plus possibly 1–2 unidentified micro-operations). This is consistent with a population of ~1.1 million in a state larger than Germany.

**Manhattan, MT is a genuine micro-cluster.** Three plastics-related manufacturers in a town of ~2,000 people: PDM (injection molding), Blackhawk/Revelyst (captive), and Montana Advanced Composites (prepregs). PDM founder Mike Groff called it "the plastic mecca of Montana." The cluster exists because of affordable land, Bozeman-area quality of life, and Montana's business-friendly environment. Note: Groff's father owns a plastic company in Ohio — the family plastics connection may explain the Manhattan genesis.

**Kalispell/Flathead Valley is Montana's second plastics node.** Northern Plastics (expanding, high-volume) and Steel Reality Molding (mold maker) form a small but active cluster in the Flathead Valley. West Paw's Columbia Falls factory (4 hours north of Bozeman) adds a third injection molding operation to the northwestern Montana corridor.

**Technology desert.** No RJG, cavity pressure monitoring, scientific molding, Moldflow, or advanced process monitoring signals were found at ANY Montana injection molder. No ISO 13485, IATF 16949, or AS9100 certifications at any Montana molder (Autopilot, the CNC shop, has AS9100 — but that's machining, not molding). This is a complete technology desert from an alliance prospecting standpoint. If any Montana company adopted advanced process monitoring, they would be the first in the state.

**Sustainability angle is distinctive.** West Paw (Zogoflex/Seaflex recycled ocean-bound plastic), Northern Plastics (bio-resin decomposition technology), and DPI Missoula (environmental commitment) all emphasize sustainability. This is likely driven by Montana's outdoor recreation culture and environmental awareness. Not a direct alliance signal, but indicates forward-thinking management cultures.

**CWP was surprisingly productive for 5 rows.** 4 unique companies, 3 turned out to be legitimate plastics operations (PDM, DPI, West Paw). Only Autopilot was a complete miss (CNC shop, not injection molding). The makeitplastic.com email domain on both PDM contacts was immediately confirmatory. DPI Missoula's diversifiedplastics.net domain resolved the MN cross-reference cleanly.

**Expected yield matched perfectly.** The hand-off predicted 0–1 HP and 1–3 Q. The result: 0 HP, 3 Q. Montana is one of the lowest-yield states in the national sweep, alongside West Virginia (2 HP, 1 Q, 1 Ref, 1 WL) and Idaho (1 HP, 4 Q, 4 WL, 1 Ref), but the three Qualified prospects are all legitimate operations with genuine scale ($10–17M revenue each).
