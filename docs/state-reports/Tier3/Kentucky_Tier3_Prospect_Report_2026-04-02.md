## Prospect Pipeline Analysis | April 2, 2026

## Executive Summary

Kentucky's plastics manufacturing base yields **6 High Priority, 9 Qualified, and 10 Watch List** prospects across a market dominated by Japanese and German captive automotive operations. The CWP dataset (74 rows, ~44 unique companies) had a **~64% exclusion rate** — the highest in the Tier 3 series — driven by Toyota (7 rows), GE Appliances (4 rows), Celanese/Ticona (4 rows), Johnson Controls (3 rows), and numerous captive foreign OEMs. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep proved critical, producing 4 of the 6 High Priority targets.

**Key finding:** Kentucky has **zero confirmed RJG eDart installations** among independent custom molders. The "RJG-without-AI" archetype translates here to "scientific-molding-discipline-without-digital-monitoring" — companies with process methodology but lacking both the sensor layer and the AI layer. This represents a greenfield opportunity for simultaneous process monitoring and AI analytics deployment.

**#1 Kentucky prospect: Par 4 Plastics (Marion, KY)** — IATF 16949-certified, family-owned custom injection molder with integrated tooling division, 600+ active tools, 30 daily mold changes, and zero AI or cavity pressure monitoring. The volume and complexity of their daily operations creates enormous optimization opportunity.

**Geographic clusters worth combining for outreach:**
- **Lexington cluster:** Blue Star Plastics + LexPlastics + Imperial Tool (3 prospects within miles, all showing process sophistication)
- **Western Kentucky:** Par 4 Plastics (Marion) + Service Tool & Plastics (Henderson) + Murray Mold & Die (Murray) — family-owned operations in underserved rural markets
- **Louisville:** HB Molding + Jones Plastic + VW Tooling Systems + Advantage Plastics

## Kentucky Market Intelligence Notes

**Zero confirmed RJG installations among independents.** This is unique among Tier 3 states surveyed. The closest match (World Class Plastics, RJG Master Molder II certified) is actually in Russells Point, Ohio, not Kentucky.

**Automotive corridor gravity.** The I-75 corridor (Florence → Georgetown) and I-65 corridor (Elizabethtown → Bowling Green) are heavily influenced by Japanese OEM transplant supply chains. Independent custom molders exist in the interstices of these corridors but are underrepresented in databases.

**Western Kentucky is underserved and promising.** Marion (Par 4), Henderson (Service Tool), Murray (Murray Mold), Corydon (Tunnell Hill, Prestige Tool) form a dispersed but genuine cluster of family-owned operations completely off the technology vendor radar.

**Lexington is the highest-density prospect cluster.** Blue Star, LexPlastics, and Imperial Tool are all within miles of each other, all demonstrating process sophistication (IQMS, scientific molding, APQP), and all lacking AI. A single trip could address three prospects.

**Dr. Schneider acquisition by Motherson (2023) is the biggest recent M&A event.** Motherson (India) acquired Dr. Schneider from insolvency for ~$132M. The Russell Springs plant has 39 machines and 240 employees. This is a captive operation but the post-acquisition integration period creates potential technology conversations.

**Marion County, KY is a "plastics city."** The county's economic development site lists plastics as a core industry, with Par 4 Plastics, Plastic Products Company (PPC), and EPC all operating within the county. This concentration suggests a local workforce pipeline familiar with injection molding.
