## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

**Mississippi's certification desert is the defining feature of this state's plastics landscape.** Zero confirmed IATF 16949, ISO 13485, AS9100, scientific molding, or RJG deployments among independent injection molders. This is extraordinary given the state hosts two major automotive OEMs (Nissan Canton, Toyota Blue Springs) and a major medical device manufacturer (Baxter Cleveland). The implication: most precision-molded parts consumed in Mississippi are sourced from out-of-state suppliers (M-Tek/TN, Kasai/TN, etc.) or captive JVs (Diversity Vuteq, APMM). An alliance engagement that helps a Mississippi molder achieve IATF 16949 or ISO 13485 certification would face essentially zero local competition.

**The DeSoto County (Memphis metro) gap is notable.** American Plastic Toys closed its 12+ press Olive Branch facility in 2024, with equipment auctioned in August 2024. No active injection molding operations were confirmed in the Memphis-adjacent DeSoto County area despite its logistics advantages and population density. This is a market gap.

**Two unidentified companies warrant immediate follow-up.** The BBB-listed injection molder at 740 Danforth Dr, Madison, MS and the Tupelo-area 18-press company for sale could meaningfully change the HP count if verified. The Tupelo company in particular — 18 presses, 30+ years, owner seeking exit — would likely score HP if confirmed as a separate entity from CEW.

**Northeast Mississippi (Tupelo corridor) is the state's plastics gravity center** with CEW (33 presses), Diversity Vuteq (24 presses), APMM (plastics division), SCP Polymers (Booneville), and possibly the unidentified 18-press company. This cluster exists because of Toyota Blue Springs.

**Mississippi Polymer Institute at USM is the connective tissue** for the state's polymer industry. Excel Injection Molding already partners with MPI. Ty Posey (CWP contact, Workforce Development Leader) is the single most valuable intelligence source for identifying additional MS molders and understanding technology adoption patterns.
