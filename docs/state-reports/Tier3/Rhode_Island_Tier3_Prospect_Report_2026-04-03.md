## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Rhode Island proved surprisingly rich for a small state — producing 4 High Priority and 2 Qualified prospects, exceeding pre-research estimates of 0–2 HP. The state's dense manufacturing base, rooted in its industrial heritage (jewelry, precision metalworking), supports an active cluster of small-to-mid-size custom injection molders in the Providence metro area and surrounding communities. The beyond-CWP sweep was especially productive, surfacing three of the four HP companies (Tri-Mack, Clayens Providence, MARS Plastics) that were not in the CWP database at all.

Key findings:
- **Fielding Manufacturing** (Cranston) — Best overall fit. Family-owned, in-house tooling, FDA registered, medical markets, 60-year legacy.
- **Tri-Mack Plastics** (Bristol) — Aerospace leader. AS9100 + Nadcap + ITAR. In-house tooling. GE Aerospace supplier. Beyond-CWP discovery.
- **Clayens Providence** (East Providence) — ISO 13485 + FDA. Acquired by French Clayens NP in June 2023 (within M&A receptivity window). 52-machine micro molding operation. Beyond-CWP discovery.
- **MARS Plastics** (Providence) — Largest independent RI molder. 40 machines, 100K sq ft, in-house tooling. No ISO certification = quality system gap opportunity. Beyond-CWP discovery.

Rhode Island borders both CT (7 HP, 9 Q in Tier 3) and MA (8 HP, 8 Q in Tier 3). No cross-tier conflicts were identified — RI prospects are distinct operations from previously scored companies.

## NOTES

- RI's beyond-CWP sweep was exceptionally productive — 3 of 4 HP companies (Tri-Mack, Clayens, MARS) were not in the CWP database. This reinforces the pattern seen across all tiers that CWP alone misses significant prospects.
- The Providence metro cluster (Cranston, East Providence, Warwick, Providence) contains nearly all RI injection molding activity. Bristol (Tri-Mack) and Woonsocket (ACW) are the outliers.
- RI's jewelry manufacturing legacy created a precision-machining workforce that translates directly to mold making and precision molding — explaining the concentration of high-capability small shops.
- Ensigner Precision Components (Germany) serves the Providence market from their Putnam, CT facility — no RI physical presence but potentially relevant competitive intelligence for RI outreach.
- No RJG or cavity pressure monitoring identified at any RI company. This represents an industry-wide gap in the state.
