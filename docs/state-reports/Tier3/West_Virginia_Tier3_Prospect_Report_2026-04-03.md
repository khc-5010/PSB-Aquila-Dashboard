## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

**West Virginia's plastics landscape is structurally upstream.** The state sits within a day's drive of 70% of U.S. polyethylene demand and 77% of U.S. polypropylene demand, with abundant Marcellus/Utica Shale feedstock (30%+ of U.S. natural gas production). But the polymer industry is concentrated in resin production and chemical manufacturing (DuPont/Chemours, Braskem, Dow, Kuraray, Momentive, Covestro), not downstream parts manufacturing. Only 3–4 confirmed injection molders operate in the entire state. This upstream/downstream disconnect means the PAZ region produces raw materials that injection molders consume nationwide, but almost none of the finished molded parts.

**The Polymer Alliance Zone (PAZ) is the key institutional player.** Created in 1996 by executive order, covering 10 Ohio River counties from Huntington to Wheeling, PAZ is actively recruiting international manufacturers. SOMAR North America (Japan) opened in 2024–2025; an unnamed Columbus-based company is establishing operations in early 2026; PAZ delegations have visited Japan, Turkey, and Europe. PAZ President Keith Burdette (304-428-1622) would be a valuable contact for early intelligence on any new injection molding entrants. The PAZ Industrial Park in Davisville (Wood County) has available land and infrastructure.

**Eastern Panhandle cross-border cluster is the densest opportunity zone.** Technimark's Inwood WV plant, combined with Blue Ridge Industries, Monoflo International, and Shadow's Ridge in Winchester VA (~30 miles away), creates a meaningful injection molding cluster straddling the WV/VA border along I-81. All four are already scored in prior reports. A regional engagement event in this area could address multiple prospects with a single trip.

**No WV injection molder holds IATF 16949, ISO 13485, AS9100, or confirmed RJG certification.** This is a "certification desert" similar to the Mississippi finding — despite automotive suppliers (K.S. of West Virginia serves auto OEMs) and medical-adjacent companies (DARCO designs medical devices) operating in the state. The absence of advanced quality certifications among WV molders may reflect the state's thin downstream base rather than a lack of sophistication.

**DARCO International Össur acquisition claim appears incorrect.** The hand-off brief stated DARCO was acquired by Össur (Iceland, now Embla Medical) in 2021. Extensive research — including DARCO's own corporate timeline, SEC filings, Össur/Embla Medical acquisition history, ZoomInfo, D&B, and news archives — found no evidence of this transaction. DARCO celebrated its independent 40th anniversary in 2025 and continues hiring and operating autonomously. This may have originated from confusion with another orthopedic brand acquisition (Össur acquired a number of smaller orthopedic companies in 2020–2022). Flagged for correction.

**Star Plastics PE trajectory is notable.** The Akoya Capital Partners acquisition (August 2021) followed by Trivalence Technologies merger (July 2025) represents an aggressive compounding platform build — WV, IN, GA, and China facilities now under one PE-backed umbrella. While not a direct prospect, the deal velocity signals PE interest in the Appalachian polymer value chain. Brian Knotts (CWP Production Manager contact) is positioned to have broad industry relationships.
