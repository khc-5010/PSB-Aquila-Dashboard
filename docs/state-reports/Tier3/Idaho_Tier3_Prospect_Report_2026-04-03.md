1. **Spokane–CdA corridor engagement strategy:** The Post Falls/CdA/Liberty Lake corridor has 7+ injection molders and mold makers within a 15-mile radius spanning the WA/ID border — including Cascadia/VRC (RJG deployed, 45 presses), ALTEK ($50M+, triple-certified), AMC (ISO 9001, active acquirer), Heppner Molds (ISO 9001, 45+ years), Summit Mold, and H&H Molds. Should this be treated as a **unified regional engagement cluster** rather than separate WA/ID outreach? A single trip to Spokane/CdA could hit 5–7 prospects.

2. **AMC's buy-and-build pattern:** Alternative Molding Concepts acquired two companies in 2021 (Dana Saad in August, EJK Plastics in October) after Coté/Vollmer bought the platform in 2019. This mirrors PE-style consolidation without confirmed PE backing. Does this acquisition pattern suggest they're building toward a PE exit, or is this organic growth? Either way, does the data integration challenge from three merged operations represent a strong engagement hook?

3. **VRC/Blue Point exit timeline:** Blue Point Capital's Fund IV (2018 vintage) is approaching its typical 7–8 year hold period, and VRC has experienced significant CEO turnover (three CEOs in 2+ years). Does an approaching PE exit make Cascadia/VRC more or less attractive for engagement? If Blue Point is preparing to sell, would AI-driven operational improvements increase portfolio value — making us a welcome conversation?

4. **Liquid silicone rubber (LSR/LIM) scope:** BPH Industries in Hayden is a liquid injection molder specializing in silicone rubber. Is LSR/LIM formally in scope for the Aquila platform? The process parameters are fundamentally different from thermoplastic injection molding (cure chemistry vs. cooling, different pressure profiles, different failure modes).

5. **Non-profit contract manufacturers:** Development Workshop Inc. in Idaho Falls is a 501(c)(3) providing vocational training through contract injection molding (90,000 sq ft, 40+ molding employees, federal contracts). Their workforce development mission aligns with PSB's educational mission. Worth a research/senior design engagement even though they're not a commercial prospect?

6. **Semiconductor supply chain opportunity:** Micron's ~$200B expansion in Boise will create massive demand for precision plastic components (wafer handling, cleanroom consumables, semiconductor packaging). No Idaho molder currently has cleanroom capability or semiconductor-grade certifications. Is this a market-development angle worth discussing with Quintex (which claims semiconductor customers and whose owner previously worked at Micron)?

## KEY INTELLIGENCE SIGNALS

### 1. Post Falls/CdA is Idaho's injection molding epicenter
Seven of Idaho's ~15 injection molding and mold-making operations are in the Post Falls/Coeur d'Alene/Hayden corridor — a 15-mile stretch that functions as an extension of the Spokane, WA manufacturing economy. Including the WA-side companies (ALTEK in Liberty Lake, H&H Molds in Spokane Valley), this corridor has 9+ plastics operations within a 30-mile radius. This concentration justifies a **dedicated corridor engagement trip** rather than isolated state-by-state outreach.

### 2. No RJG deployment in any Idaho-only company
Cascadia/VRC (cross-tier from WA) is the only entity in the Idaho market with confirmed RJG cavity-pressure monitoring. No Idaho-headquartered company has adopted RJG or any comparable cavity-pressure technology. This represents a **technology desert** for process monitoring — similar to the finding in Oregon. The opportunity is greenfield but adoption barriers may be high.

### 3. AMC's three-company consolidation is the best discovery
Alternative Molding Concepts' acquisition of EJK Plastics (40-year mold maker) and Dana Saad within 3 months in 2021, combined with ISO 9001 certification and diversified market mix, makes this the **strongest Idaho-native prospect.** The data integration pain from three merged operations is exactly the kind of problem the alliance solves.

### 4. Idaho's certification landscape is thin
Only 3 companies in the entire Idaho injection molding market hold ISO certifications: Cascadia/VRC (AS9100D, ISO 9001, IATF 16949, ISO 13485 — via VRC parent), AMC (ISO 9001:2015), and Heppner Molds (ISO 9001:2015). No Idaho-native company holds medical (ISO 13485), aerospace (AS9100), or automotive (IATF 16949) certifications. This limits near-term engagement to companies where regulatory/certification pressure isn't the driver — the value proposition needs to focus on operational efficiency, knowledge capture, and tooling optimization rather than compliance.

### 5. Micron's $200B expansion is a long-term catalyst
Micron's massive Boise expansion (two new DRAM fabs, first output ~2027, 17,000+ jobs projected) will eventually require local precision plastics suppliers — but the current Idaho molder base lacks the certifications, cleanroom capability, and scale to serve semiconductor customers. This is a 3–5 year horizon opportunity, not an immediate engagement trigger.

### 6. CWP database had highest exclusion-to-useful ratio yet
Of 6 CWP rows (5 unique companies), only 3 are legitimate injection molding prospects (Summit Mold, Quintex, Cascadia/VRC cross-tier). BPH is LSR niche, PVC Spiral Supply is an extruder. The CWP hit rate in Idaho (~50% useful) is among the lowest of any state analyzed — the beyond-CWP sweep was essential for building a meaningful prospect picture.

### 7. Schweitzer Engineering Laboratories — captive molder worth monitoring
SEL (Pullman WA / Moscow-Lewiston ID) is a 5,000+ employee, 100% ESOP power grid protection manufacturer with captive injection molding. They're actively hiring moldmakers ($32.69/hr) and mold operators ($28/hr), indicating expanding molding capacity. While captive and not a direct prospect, their ESOP structure, ISO 9001, and growing operations in the Moscow-Lewiston corridor could make them a future research partner or workforce development collaborator.
