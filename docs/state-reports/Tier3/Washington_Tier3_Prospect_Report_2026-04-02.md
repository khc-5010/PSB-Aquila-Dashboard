## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Washington's injection molding ecosystem is smaller and more fragmented than Midwest or Great Lakes states, but it harbors several high-value independent prospects alongside a wave of recent PE and foreign acquisitions. Of 20 CWP database companies investigated, only 6 are active custom injection molders — and 3 of those have already been acquired by PE platforms. The beyond-CWP discovery sweep uncovered 12+ additional companies, including two elite Boeing-certified molders and one of the Pacific Northwest's largest vertically integrated operations.

**Key findings:**
- **3 High Priority** independent prospects with multiple strong readiness signals
- **5 Medium-High Priority** companies with strong operations but acquisition or scale caveats
- **7 Qualified** companies worth monitoring or tracking
- **Critical corrections:** Vaupell is owned by Sumitomo Bakelite (NOT HEICO); Carlisle CIT sold to Amphenol for $2.025B; KeyTronic ticker is KTCC not KTEC; "Superion Plastics" is actually Superlon (pipe extruder, not molder)
- **Top independent prospect:** Altek Inc. (Liberty Lake) — family-owned, 150 employees, ISO 13485 + AS9100 + ITAR, in-house tooling, largest press in PNW, clean room, IME capability
- **Time-sensitive opportunity:** DEMGY Pacific (formerly Tool Gauge, Tacoma) — acquired March 2025 by French DEMGY Group, ~200 employees, Tier 1 Boeing supplier, sitting in the optimal 6–12 month post-acquisition receptivity window
