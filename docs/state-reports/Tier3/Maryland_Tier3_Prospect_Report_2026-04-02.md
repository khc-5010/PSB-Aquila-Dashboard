## NOTES

### Maryland's injection molding landscape is structurally thin
The total confirmed independent injection molding universe in Maryland is approximately 10–12 companies with manufacturing operations. This is among the thinnest state markets in the Mid-Atlantic, explained by Maryland's economic structure: the DC-adjacent economy favors government, defense contracting, biotech R&D, and professional services over traditional manufacturing. The plastics processing that does exist concentrates in two narrow corridors: Harford County I-95 (DWE, Old Federal Tool, Maryland Plastic Products, Innovative Plastic Solutions — 4 companies within 15 miles) and Baltimore County (Fawn Industries, Cardinal Plastics — 2 companies within 10 miles). Everything else is scattered individual shops.

### Harford County I-95 cluster is the engagement strategy
Four prospects — DWE Inc. (HP), Old Federal Tool (HP), Maryland Plastic Products (Q), and Innovative Plastic Solutions (WL) — are located within a 15-minute drive of each other along the I-95/Route 1 corridor in Harford County. This density warrants a cluster outreach approach: a single trip to Bel Air/Belcamp/Forest Hill could touch three or four prospects in a day. The cluster also sits adjacent to Aberdeen Proving Ground, creating a shared defense supply chain context.

### Zero RJG/eDart adoption across the entire Maryland market
No company in the Maryland pipeline showed RJG, eDart, or cavity-pressure monitoring signals. DWE's scientific molding + SPC is the closest thing to process monitoring infrastructure. This means the entire state market is greenfield territory for the Alliance's core "data-rich, insight-poor" pitch — but it also means the pitch needs to be adjusted. Instead of "we add intelligence to your existing monitoring data," the conversation for most MD prospects starts earlier: "let's look at what process data you're already generating and how AI can extract value from it."

### The mold/tool maker desert creates an ecosystem opportunity
Only two dedicated mold/tool shops serve all of Maryland: Old Federal Tool Company (Forest Hill) and KK Mold Design (Keymar). Maryland injection molders overwhelmingly outsource tooling — likely to Pennsylvania, Ohio, or offshore. This creates a potential ecosystem play: the Alliance's tooling optimization offering could help Maryland molders get more value from their outsourced tooling relationships by providing data-driven feedback to their mold suppliers.

### Blow molding sector is actually larger than injection molding in parts of Maryland
Tim Plastics (North East, 201–500 employees, 520,000+ sq ft), Parker Plastics (Hagerstown, 100,000+ sq ft, multi-plant), and Plastipak Packaging (Havre de Grace, PET blow molding) represent substantial plastics manufacturing outside the injection molding pipeline. If the Alliance expands beyond injection molding, these would be priority targets.

### Western Maryland and I-270 biotech corridor are complete deserts
Extensive searching of Frederick, Hagerstown, Cumberland, Gaithersburg, Rockville, and Silver Spring produced zero injection molders or mold makers. The biotech companies in the I-270 corridor outsource all plastics manufacturing to other states (PA, NJ, DE). Western Maryland has some blow molding (Parker Plastics in Hagerstown) but no injection molding.

### Scope questions for Brett Hyder
