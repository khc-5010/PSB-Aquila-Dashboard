# Indiana plastics prospect pipeline: deep signal analysis for PSB–Aquila Alliance

The research across 35 Indiana-based companies identified **9 High Priority targets**, **8 Qualified prospects**, **10 Watch List companies**, and **8 non-fits** (including one closure, duplicates, and off-category businesses). The strongest overall prospects are **SRG Global/RESRG Automotive** (confirmed RJG partner with April 2025 M&A), **Genesis Plastics/Viking Plastics** (deepest Penn State Behrend alumni network of any target), and **ICU Medical/Smiths Medical Gary** (confirmed scientific injection molding in a validated medical environment with recent $2.35B acquisition). Three ESOP-owned companies — American Plastic Molding, Manar Inc, and B&B Molders — form a cluster of mid-market converters with scientific molding signals and employee-driven investment appetite.

## Penn State Behrend connections are concentrated in two companies

Only **two companies** in the entire 35-company pipeline have confirmed Penn State Behrend connections. **Viking Plastics** (Genesis Plastics parent) has at least 5 employees with PSB degrees including a Process Engineer with a Plastics Engineering Technology degree, plus AIM Institute/John Beaumont lineage and a Corry, PA HQ 30 miles from campus. **Samtec** actively recruits PSB Plastics/Polymer Engineering co-ops and attends Penn State Erie career fairs. The remaining 33 companies show stronger Purdue and Ball State connections, consistent with Indiana geography. This suggests PSB outreach to most Indiana targets will require cold-start relationship building rather than leveraging existing ties — making Viking Plastics and Samtec valuable anchor points for warm introductions and credibility building in the state.

## Ownership patterns reveal engagement strategy implications

The pipeline's ownership distribution shapes the engagement approach. Three companies are **ESOP-owned** (American Plastic Molding, Manar, and arguably B&B Molders' management-buyout structure), which correlates with faster technology adoption decisions and employee-driven investment appetite. Five companies are **PE-backed** (Viking/Spell Capital, SRG Global/Atlas Holdings, Exo-s/Desjardins, Fort Wayne Plastics/American Plastics LLC, and TomKen/QMI Group), creating the classic 3–5 year value-creation window where operational technology investments are prioritized. Four are **corporate subsidiaries** of large multinationals (King Systems/Ambu A/S, ICU Medical, GECOM/Hi-Lex ACT, Paragon/AMETEK) where engagement must navigate corporate decision-making but larger budgets are available. The balance are **family-owned** operations across generational transitions, where succession planning and knowledge capture create natural entry points for Industrial AI conversations.
