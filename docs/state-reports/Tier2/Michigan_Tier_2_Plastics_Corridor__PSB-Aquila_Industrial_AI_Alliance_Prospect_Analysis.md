# Michigan Tier 2 prospect deep research for the PSB-Aquila Industrial AI Alliance

**Michigan's plastics corridor offers 15–18 viable alliance prospects out of 40 companies researched, but five closures since 2022 reveal an industry in painful transition — exactly the conditions that create urgent demand for AI-driven process intelligence.** The strongest prospects share a common profile: decades of accumulated tooling and process data, confirmed scientific molding or cavity pressure monitoring, and impending leadership transitions that threaten institutional knowledge. Three companies — PTI Engineered Plastics, Ironwood Plastics, and Anderson Technologies — already run RJG eDart systems, making them textbook "data-rich, insight-poor" targets. Several others demonstrate cavity pressure monitoring, scientific molding, and custom ERP systems that generate data streams ripe for AI layer integration.

The research uncovered a striking pattern: **five companies on this list have closed since 2022** (CRW Plastics, Master Precision Mold, West Michigan Molding, Proper Tooling, and Leon Plastics' US operations). Three of those closures involved PE mismanagement or financial distress. This attrition concentrates surviving companies' workloads, intensifies their hiring challenges, and makes the case for process optimization tools more urgent than ever.

### 1. PTI Engineered Plastics — the ideal alliance archetype

**Macomb, MI** · teampti.com · **Converter + In-House Tooling**
**~300 employees · Founded 1984 · 44+ presses (12–750T) · ~$50M+ revenue**

PTI is the single strongest prospect on this list. Founded by career mold maker Mark Rathbone, the company was literally built on toolmaking and retains **40 highly skilled mold makers** operating 25 CNC machining centers, 5-axis mills, and robotic-fed EDM cells. They are a **certified RJG mold builder and tryout facility**, running eDart process monitoring with cavity pressure sensors and practicing Decoupled III molding — the exact "RJG without AI" profile the alliance prioritizes.

PTI's medical device work is extensive: **ISO 13485, FDA-registered, MedAccred-accredited** (endorsed for Medtronic, BD, Boston Scientific, Stryker, and other tier-one OEMs), with 10,000+ sq ft of ISO Class 8 cleanroom recently tripled in capacity. They also hold **IATF 16949** and are ITAR-registered. Their technology stack includes Moldflow simulation, collaborative robots on the production floor, vision systems, and a Conair Industry 4.0 material handling system. Named **2023 Plastics News Processor of the Year**.

**Critical vulnerability: founder succession risk.** Mark Rathbone (50+ years in the industry) remains sole owner and active CEO with no publicly identified successor. Many employees have 30+ years of tenure. This is precisely the "retiring expertise" risk that creates urgency for knowledge capture and AI-assisted process documentation.

- **Industries:** Medical devices (primary), defense/aerospace, automotive, electronics, consumer
- **Certifications:** ISO 13485, IATF 16949, MedAccred, FDA-registered, UL, ITAR
- **Technology signals:** ✅ RJG eDart, ✅ Scientific molding (DIII), ✅ Moldflow, ✅ Cobots, ✅ Industry 4.0 material handling, ✅ DOE/capability studies
- **Ownership:** Founder-owned private company, not PE-backed
- **PSB connection:** None found, but strong technical alignment with Behrend PET curriculum and RJG Master Molder program
- **Alliance fit: HIGH PRIORITY** — Checks every box: RJG without AI, 40-year mold library, in-house tooling + molding, medical-regulated, retiring expertise risk, founder-owned

## Five closures expose the industry's deepest vulnerability

The most sobering finding in this research is the **pattern of Michigan plastics company closures**: CRW Plastics (2022), Proper Tooling (2023), West Michigan Molding (2024), and Master Precision Mold (2024), plus Leon Plastics' US exit (2015). Proper Tooling's collapse is particularly instructive — once the #7 North American toolmaker with $84M revenue and 280 employees, it was destroyed when PE firm New Water Capital reneged on a purchase agreement, leaving the company with unsustainable debt.

These closures have displaced hundreds of experienced moldmakers and process engineers into the remaining company ecosystem. For surviving companies, this represents both a talent acquisition opportunity and an acceleration of the very workforce challenges that make AI-driven knowledge capture urgent. **Every company that closes takes decades of institutional tooling and process knowledge with it.** The alliance's value proposition — preserving and digitizing that knowledge before it walks out the door — has never been more relevant.

## The "RJG without AI" opportunity is concentrated and actionable

Three companies are confirmed RJG eDart users: **PTI Engineered Plastics, Ironwood Plastics, and Anderson Technologies.** At least two more (TH Plastics and Sturgis Molded Products) describe cavity pressure monitoring in terms consistent with RJG or equivalent systems. These five companies are generating rich, structured process data from every shot cycle — pressure curves, temperature profiles, fill/pack/hold parameters — without an AI analytics layer interpreting that data at scale.

This represents the alliance's most direct, quantifiable value proposition: these companies already have the sensors, already have the data infrastructure, and already understand the value of scientific molding. They need the AI layer that transforms millions of data points into predictive quality models, automated process optimization, and real-time anomaly detection. The sales conversation shifts from "you need to invest in sensors" to "you already have the data — let us unlock its value."

## Penn State Behrend connections remain an untapped asset

**No direct Penn State or Penn State Behrend connections were found for any of the 40 companies researched.** This is both a gap and an opportunity. The strongest indirect connection point is through RJG technology — PSB's Plastics Training Academy hosts RJG Master Molder certification, and several target companies use RJG systems. This shared technology platform could serve as a natural bridge for introductions and credibility-building in the Michigan market. The alliance should consider whether Michigan plastics industry events (SPE ANTEC, Plastics News conferences, MAPP meetings) could create connection opportunities — Craig Carrel of Team 1 Plastics sits on the MAPP board, for instance.
