# Illinois plastics prospect pipeline for the Behrend–Aquila AI Alliance

The Greater Chicago plastics corridor yields **15 high-value prospects** from the 55 companies screened, with two standout "RJG without AI" targets — **Fabrik Molded Plastics** (confirmed RJG/eDart user) and **Wise Plastics Technologies** (scientific/decoupled molding, ISO 13485, 350K+ sq ft). Hoffer Plastics emerges as the single most impressive operation overall, fresh off an IndustryWeek Best Plants award. The scan also uncovered several legacy mold shops undergoing founder retirements and PE acquisitions — classic engagement windows — and three enterprise-scale targets (Aptar, Logoplaste, Pactiv/Novolex) that could serve as flagship anchor partners. Of the 55 companies investigated, 8 were flagged as non-converters, closed, relocated, or dissolved, and another 12+ are too small or lack sufficient signals for near-term pursuit.

---

## The 15 priority prospects, ranked by alliance readiness

The ranking weights confirmed process monitoring technology, legacy mold library depth, leadership transitions, recent capital investment, medical/automotive certifications, and independence (non-PE ownership is preferred for pilot flexibility, though PE targets offer scale). Each full profile follows below.

## 1. Fabrik Molded Plastics — McHenry, IL

**Website:** https://www.fabrikind.com/ | **7 CWP contacts** | **Founded 1980** | **~200–300 employees** | **80 injection molding presses**

Fabrik is the single most "RJG without AI" company in this pipeline. Founded by Seth Wagner with two machines and a small tool shop, the company has grown to **80 presses (64 horizontal, 16 vertical)** across 160,000 sq ft in two McHenry facilities, running predominantly ENGEL machines with Arburg and Toshiba units. They serve automotive (~50% of revenue, especially lighting/lenses), medical, electronics, and consumer markets.

**Confirmed readiness signals:** Fabrik's scientific molding page explicitly states they use **RJG in-mold pressure sensors and the eDart monitoring system** to monitor processes and automatically contain out-of-spec product. This is the exact "RJG without AI" profile the alliance targets. They also practice **scientific injection molding** as a branded core capability, run **custom automation** from a dedicated 4,000 sq ft development area, offer CT scanning services, and perform reshoring/tooling transfers with reverse engineering. The company holds **ISO/TS 16949** (automotive) certification.

**Legacy and leadership signals:** Now a **3rd-generation family business** — Keith Wagner serves as CEO, succeeding founder Seth Wagner. This generational transition, combined with 45+ years of operations serving high-mix automotive, medical, and electronics customers, points to a substantial legacy mold library. Fabrik exhibited at **PTXPO 2025** and has invested in all-electric Arburg machines.

**Penn State connections:** None found directly, but Fabrik has hosted interns from Pittsburg State University's plastics program and was actively seeking additional university partnerships per a Plastics News interview — a clear door-opener for Behrend outreach.

**Engagement type:** Pilot (RJG/eDart data integration + AI layer) | **Next step:** Direct outreach to Keith Wagner referencing their RJG/eDart infrastructure and Behrend's plastics engineering program. Position as "adding the AI layer to the monitoring foundation you already have."

## Conclusion: two clear pilot candidates and a rich secondary pipeline

The scan's most actionable finding is that **Fabrik Molded Plastics is an almost-perfect first engagement** — confirmed RJG/eDart infrastructure, scientific molding culture, 3rd-generation transition, university partnership interest, and 7 CWP contacts creating multiple warm introduction paths. **Wise Plastics Technologies** is the strongest single-CWP discovery, offering scientific/decoupled molding at significant scale ($35M, 346 employees) with medical and defense certifications but no PE complications. **Hoffer Plastics** may ultimately be the most impactful partnership given their IndustryWeek Best Plant status and IIoT infrastructure, though their existing sophistication means the value proposition must be framed as advanced analytics rather than foundational monitoring.

Among mold makers, **Industrial Molds/Pyramid Molding Group** stands out for lights-out automation and vertical integration, while **Pelco Tool and Mold** and **Helm Tool** represent classic founder-era shops where institutional knowledge preservation is an urgent, concrete need. The March 2026 expansion at **Group Tool** and the Industry 4.0 data collection plans at **AMT Plastics** create time-sensitive engagement triggers that should be acted on within the next 60–90 days.

The Illinois pipeline's distinctive characteristic relative to other geographies is its **mold maker density** — the Elk Grove Village/Addison/Schaumburg corridor alone contained 8+ active or recently-transitioned tool shops. Several of these are undergoing founder retirements and PE acquisitions simultaneously, creating a narrow but high-value window for positioning the alliance as a solution for preserving and digitizing decades of accumulated tooling expertise before it walks out the door.
