## NOTES

### Regional Patterns Observed
- **Meadville, PA cluster is the most strategically significant finding.** C&J Industries, Allegheny Performance Plastics (via Holbrook), Quality Tool & Die / QTD Plastics, and Jamestown Plastics (via Tamarack) all have operations within miles of each other — and only 15 miles from PSB campus. This concentration creates opportunity for a Meadville manufacturing AI cohort where multiple companies share learning and infrastructure.
- **Northeast Ohio's Akron-to-Ashtabula corridor** holds the largest absolute number of targets, anchored by Ferriot (Akron), Thogus (Avon Lake), and Venture Plastics (Newton Falls). Farther from campus, so engagement models need to be more structured (quarterly on-site visits + remote support).
- **RJG deployment without AI** emerged as the single clearest readiness indicator. Venture Plastics (full deployment, 36 machines) and Matrix Tool (full deployment, 42 machines) both generate rich cavity-pressure datasets used only for basic process control and part sorting. The gap between data collection and data intelligence is the Alliance's exact value proposition.
- **Third-generation family transitions** appear at Matrix Tool (5 grandsons), Confer Plastics (Bob Confer), Thogus (Matt Hlavin), and Jamestown Plastics (Jessup Baker). These create 12-24 month windows where new leadership is most receptive to technology partnerships.
- **Medical device convergence** across at least 8 profiled companies (C&J, Allegheny/Holbrook, Jamestown, Caplugs/Medbio, Thogus/JALEX, Quality Tool/QTD, Accudyn, and others). ISO 13485 compliance demands traceability, validation, and process control documentation that AI dramatically improves. A medical device plastics AI workshop targeting this cluster could generate multiple partnerships from a single event.

### Contextual Observations
- **Beaumont Technologies as a bridge.** Beaumont Technologies (Erie, PA) provides Moldflow simulation and training to multiple targets including Venture Plastics. John Beaumont's relationship with the Erie plastics community could facilitate introductions to companies using his simulation services — they are pre-qualified as data-aware manufacturers.
- **The ESOP advantage at C&J Industries.** Employee-owned companies invest in workforce development because every employee is an owner — this aligns perfectly with the Alliance's training and knowledge-capture value propositions. C&J's public advocacy for the ESOP model and their existing state workforce development grant create a natural funding pathway.
- **The "data-rich, insight-poor" pattern.** The most common finding across all targets is companies that have invested in data-generating infrastructure (RJG sensors, ERP systems, Moldflow simulations, CMMs) but lack any analytical layer that turns data into actionable intelligence. The Alliance should lead with this framing: "You already have the data. We help you use it."
