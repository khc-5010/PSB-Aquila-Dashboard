# Alliance Industry Infrastructure Company Profiles

The injection molding industry's infrastructure layer — the companies that train, equip, monitor, and standardize the work of every converter and mold maker on the planet — is controlled by a remarkably small number of organizations. Across three categories (knowledge/training, catalog/standards, and hot runner systems), roughly **20 companies service an industry worth hundreds of billions of dollars**. This report profiles the six Brett-identified targets in depth, surfaces an additional 15+ companies worth tracking, and assesses each for alliance fit as a customer, strategic partner, or both. The single most important finding: **RJG Inc. already has a deep, formal partnership with Penn State Behrend** and is building AI capabilities (MAX) that the alliance could augment — making them the most natural first conversation in this entire category.

**CWP Database Cross-Reference (March 2026):** Michelle Hortmann's Continuing Workforce Programs contact database was cross-checked against all profiled companies. Results are embedded in each company's PSB connection section. The CWP data revealed significantly deeper PSB relationships than web research alone — notably at Husky (10+ contacts), DME (5 contacts including the Director of Engineering), INCOE (6 contacts), and Kistler (4 contacts including two regional plastics managers based near Erie).

## CATEGORY A: KNOWLEDGE SECTOR

### RJG Inc. — The industry's scientific molding standard-bearer

**Website:** https://rjginc.com | **HQ:** Traverse City, MI | **Founded:** 1985 | **Employees:** ~170 | **Revenue:** ~$15–39M | **Ownership:** Private, founder/family-owned

RJG is the undisputed global leader in scientific injection molding training, process monitoring technology, and consulting. Founded by Rod Groleau, the company pioneered **DECOUPLED MOLDING®** — the methodology now taught as foundational practice worldwide — and created the industry's gold-standard credential, the **Master Molder certification** (Levels I, II, III). Their business integrates three pillars: training programs delivered globally at 12+ locations, the **CoPilot® process monitoring system** (successor to the retired eDART platform) with the new **MAX AI process advisor**, and consulting services including TZERO® art-to-part optimization. Their proprietary Lynx™ smart sensors and The Hub® centralized data server complete an ecosystem that spans from the sensor in the mold cavity to the cloud.

**Data profile.** RJG possesses one of the most unique data assets in plastics. Their CoPilot/eDART installed base has collected **millions of cavity pressure curves** across decades — the raw physics of how plastic behaves inside molds, tagged by material, machine, part geometry, and process parameters. The Hub stores process templates and historical data. Their training records span thousands of certified professionals. The MAX AI was "trained on data from multiple sources, including the company's in-house CoPilot data and the expertise of its trainers." However, most customer process data resides at customer sites — RJG likely does not have centralized access unless customers opt in. Data quality from instrumented systems is **very high** (machine-generated, calibrated sensors, automated timestamps).

**Industry position.** Near-monopoly in advanced injection molding certification. No competitor approaches Master Molder's recognition — it appears as a requirement in job postings nationwide. Jabil's internal Molding Institute teaches RJG's curriculum. In process monitoring, CoPilot competes with Kistler's ComoNeo and Priamus's FILLCONTROL, but RJG's integration of sensors + monitoring + training + AI is unmatched.

**AI and digital initiatives.** RJG is **already building AI**. MAX (Molding Automation Xperience) provides step-by-step troubleshooting guidance powered by Master Molder techniques, running locally on CoPilot hardware. The transition from eDART to CoPilot represents a major platform modernization. They have an eDART Migration Tool for installed base conversion. However, MAX is relatively narrow (troubleshooting guidance only) — there is significant headroom for broader AI applications across their data ecosystem.

**PSB connection: CONFIRMED AND DEEP.** Penn State Behrend was the **first location outside RJG headquarters** to offer the Master Molder Certification series. RJG has been **consigning equipment to Behrend's plastics processing lab for several years**. Brad Johnson (Lecturer in Plastics Engineering Technology at Behrend) is the primary liaison. Robert Simoneau (former Director, Behrend School of Engineering) publicly endorsed the partnership. **CWP database cross-check: 9 entries / 7 unique contacts** — including **Doug Espinoza** (Director of Partnership Development) and **Matt Groleau** (Chief Analytics Officer, founder's son). Other contacts: Dan Clark, Ken Hermann, Bob Reese, Thom Smith, Jeremy Williams, Brandon Birchmeier. This is not a casual connection — it's a sustained, multi-person relationship spanning training, equipment, and leadership.

**Key contacts.** Rob Nugent (President & CEO, newly appointed — explicitly tasked with scaling globally); Michael Groleau (Principal & Chief Analytics Officer — founder's son, **in CWP database**, signals data/analytics as strategic priority); Doug Espinoza (Director of Partnership Development — his role is literally to develop partnerships, **in CWP database**); Jason Yoffy (Director of Product — owns MAX, CoPilot, technology roadmap).

**Alliance angle: BOTH — customer and strategic partner.** The strongest play positions the alliance as helping RJG enhance their own analytics capabilities (feeding into MAX and The Hub) while simultaneously using RJG's brand and installed base as a go-to-market channel. RJG's CoPilot/Hub network is already a data pipeline; adding advanced AI/analytics on top creates enormous value. Their new CEO is explicitly looking to "take RJG to the next level." The existing PSB relationship provides a warm introduction path — and the CWP database gives direct contact information for the two ideal entry points (Espinoza and Groleau). **Customer overlap is essentially 100%** — anyone doing serious injection molding is already an RJG customer or prospect.

**Engagement approach.** Lead with the AI/data story — RJG has MAX but has barely scratched the surface. Frame as augmenting their platform, not replacing it. Emphasize the data aggregation opportunity (today each customer's data is siloed; the alliance could help create an anonymized "industry brain"). Use the Penn State Behrend connection as a warm entry. Target Rob Nugent (CEO) and Michael Groleau (Chief Analytics Officer) first, then Doug Espinoza (Partnership Development). The CWP relationship means Brad Johnson at PSB could facilitate introductions.

## CATEGORY B: CATALOG / STANDARDS COMPANIES

### DME Company — The American mold standard since 1942

**Website:** https://www.dme.net | **HQ:** Harrison Township, MI (Detroit metro) | **Founded:** 1942 | **Employees:** ~326 US (likely 500-800+ globally) | **Revenue:** ~$163M (estimate) | **Ownership:** Hillenbrand Inc. (NYSE: HI) — Molding Technology Solutions segment

DME Company (originally Detroit Mold Engineering) is the premier North American supplier of standardized mold components, mold bases, and melt delivery systems. Their catalog of **35,000+ items** has been described as "the de facto bible for the mold making industry" — the DME numbering system is the reference specification used in mold design guidelines worldwide. Products span mold bases (A, B, X, AX, T-Series, MUD® quick-change), components (ejector pins, sleeves, blades, core pins, sprue bushings, guide bushings, leader pins, parting line locks), cooling systems (Flosense digital monitoring), and — notably — **hot runner systems** (SmartONE, StellarONE, EcoONE-Series) and temperature controllers (SmartSeries up to 216 zones). DME definitively **spans Categories B and C**.

**Data profile.** DME has one of the richest digitized data assets in mold components. Their **CAD Parts Library** contains full 3D parametric models for their entire 35,000+ catalog, with native integrations for SolidWorks, Siemens NX, STEP, Parasolid, DXF, and DWG formats. The PARTcommunity online portal, offline 3D CAD Library app, and Mold Base Configurator provide multiple access paths. **This data is deeply embedded in designer workflows** — DME component libraries ship inside IMOLD (SolidWorks), Siemens NX Mold Wizard, and other leading mold design packages. Their eSTORE captures rich purchasing pattern data. Their SmartMold IoT monitoring system and Flosense digital cooling monitors generate emerging operational data streams. Data quality is **high** — decades of parametric standardization.

**Ownership chain (critical).** Founded 1942 → Acquired by Milacron (1996) → Milacron bankruptcy and reorganization (2009) → Hillenbrand acquired Milacron for ~$1.9B (2019) → Hillenbrand sold 51% of Milacron machinery business to Bain Capital for $287M (Feb 2025) but **retained 100% of DME, Mold-Masters, and Tirad**. DME and Mold-Masters are now sister companies under Hillenbrand's MTS segment. Hillenbrand CEO: Kim Ryan.

**Industry position.** One of three dominant global mold component standards alongside HASCO (European) and MISUMI (Japanese). DME's imperial/American standard is the default specification for mold construction across North America.

**PSB connection: CONFIRMED VIA CWP DATABASE.** Web research found no direct PSB relationship, but the CWP database tells a different story. **5 contacts including a key leadership target:** **Michael Rayman** (Director of Engineering and Innovations — the exact person the deep research identified as a key contact), **Michael Kreitner** (Business Manager), **Michael Leibfritz** (Product Designer), **Moira Mehler** (Regional Sales Manager, Madison Heights MI), and **Neil Beezer**. Additionally, **Miki Bogar** appears under a combined "Mold-Masters & DME" listing, confirming the cross-brand Hillenbrand connection. DME previously operated a Pennsylvania plant in Youngwood (~150 miles south of Erie, closed 2017).

**Key contacts.** Peter R. Smith (President, DME); Chevy Herbst (COO); **Michael Rayman** (Director of Engineering — **in CWP database**); Scott Smith (Director of Sales).

**Alliance angle: Primarily strategic partner with secondary customer value.** DME's embedded position in mold designer workflows is extraordinarily powerful for distribution. If the alliance's data ontology aligns with DME's component standards, it creates a bridge directly into every customer's design environment. Their IoT products (SmartMold, Flosense) generate AI-ready data that is likely underutilized. The Hillenbrand parent connection means a single corporate relationship could encompass both DME and Mold-Masters — the mold component standard AND the hot runner standard under one roof. The CWP database contact with Michael Rayman (Director of Engineering) provides a direct warm path.

### Hasco — The European mold standard since 1924

**Website:** https://www.hasco.com | **HQ:** Lüdenscheid, Germany | **Founded:** 1924 | **Employees:** ~700-1,100 | **Revenue:** $100-500M range (estimated) | **Ownership:** Berndorf AG (Austrian industrial conglomerate, majority since 2007)

HASCO invented and patented the modular standard mold system in 1960 — literally defining international standards for mold construction. They manufacture over **100,000 standardized products** including mold bases, standard components, hot runner systems (including the innovative 3D-printed **Streamrunner** manifold), and hot runner controllers (Primezone series). Their digital catalog offers CAD data in **40+ formats** via the HASCO Portal, HASCO SET offline design tool, and CAD Direct Insert Drivers for all major systems. HASCO also offers mold tracking technology: **Mold Track** (ultra-wideband indoor localization), **Loc Check** (GPS tracker), and **RFID tagging** embedded in components for digital product history and anti-counterfeiting.

**Data profile.** Over 100 years of mold component design data, 100,000+ parametric products in 40+ CAD formats, increasingly rich IoT data from RFID-tagged components and tracking systems. Like DME, HASCO's component libraries are natively embedded in major CAD platforms — Autodesk Inventor's Mold Design module includes HASCO as a built-in standard library. Data quality is **very high** — structured, parametric, standardized over decades.

**PSB connection: CONFIRMED — Conference Gold Sponsor + CWP contact.** HASCO America was a **Gold Sponsor of Penn State Behrend's 2022 Innovation & Emerging Plastics Technologies Conference** — a significant commercial relationship. **CWP database: 1 contact** — Jay Fiodorra (HASCO America, personal email on file — may be former employee). HASCO's US office is in Fletcher, NC (~500 miles from Erie).

**Key contacts.** Christoph Ehrlich (CEO since 2008); Brenda Clark (Engineering Manager, Hasco America — best US entry point).

**Alliance angle: Strategic partner.** HASCO's 100,000+ parametric products in 40+ CAD formats represent a massive structured data asset. Their RFID tagging, UWB tracking, and online configurator demonstrate digital investment that AI/analytics could amplify. Their PSB conference sponsorship provides a warm introduction path.

### Progressive Components — The mold monitoring pioneer

**Website:** https://www.procomps.com | **HQ:** Wauconda, IL | **Founded:** ~1990 | **Employees:** ~50-100 | **Revenue:** $10-50M (estimated) | **Ownership:** Private, family-owned (Don Starkey Jr. and Glenn Starkey, 50/50)

Progressive Components is unique because they span **two categories**: standard mold components AND mold monitoring/tracking technology. On the component side, they make lifters (UniLifters), slide retainers, alignment locks, ejector pins, and cooling components — all Made in USA. On the monitoring side, they offer the **CVe Monitor RT** (real-time Bluetooth mold monitor tracking cycle counts, uptime/downtime, temperatures), **CVe Access Monitor** (for large fleets), and the **ProFile v5 cloud-based platform** (unified asset management, real-time KPI reporting, mold health monitoring, GPS tracking via QR-coded asset tags, mobile app). They essentially created the mold monitoring category with the first patented industry cycle counter in 1994.

**Data profile.** Progressive's monitoring data is the unique asset — **30+ years of cycle counting and mold performance data** across thousands of deployed devices worldwide. ProFile v5 collects real-time IoT data streams (cycle counts, uptime/downtime, cycle times, temperatures, GPS locations). This time-series data is highly structured and AI-ready. Their component catalog data is standard but smaller than DME or HASCO.

**PSB connection: MINIMAL.** **CWP database: 1 contact** — Scott Kovalska. AMBA membership provides network overlap. Progressive sponsors the AMBA Mold Builder of the Year Award.

**Key contacts.** Glenn Starkey (Owner, President — also Director of Engineering and Sales); Peter Friddle (COO); Sujit Sheth (Monitoring Sales).

**Alliance angle: Strategic partner — especially on monitoring/data.** ProFile generates real-time operational data from thousands of molds worldwide — a treasure trove for predictive maintenance, tool life optimization, and production scheduling algorithms. As a small, independent, family-owned company, decision-making is fast with no corporate layers. Their monitoring data combined with the alliance's AI capabilities could create a compelling joint product. **This is the most approachable company in Category B** due to its size, independence, and data-forward positioning.

## CATEGORY C: HOT RUNNER SYSTEM COMPANIES

### Husky Technologies — The PET packaging giant with massive digital infrastructure

**Website:** https://www.husky.co | **HQ:** Bolton, Ontario, Canada | **Founded:** 1953 | **Employees:** 4,300+ | **Revenue:** ~$1.2-1.9B | **Ownership:** GPGI Inc. (NYSE: GPGI) since January 2026

Husky Technologies is the undisputed global leader in PET preform injection molding systems and one of two dominant hot runner manufacturers. Their product portfolio is vast: **hot runner systems** (Ultra™, UltraShot™ servo-driven, UNIFY, Pronto™ with 100+ manifold layouts and online configurator), **injection molding machines** (HyPET®6e for PET preforms, HyCAP™4 for caps, HyPERSYNC™ for thin-wall packaging, ICHOR™ for medical), **Altanium® mold controllers** with DataWave 2.0 technology, and complete integrated production systems. Every major beverage company's PET supply chain touches Husky equipment — their **13,000+ systems operate in 140+ countries**.

**Ownership history is important.** Founded 1953 by Robert Schad → Onex Corporation (2007) → Berkshire Partners/OMERS for ~$2.1B (2011) → **Platinum Equity for $3.85B** (2018) → **GPGI Inc. for ~$5.0B enterprise value** (January 2026). The transition from PE to a publicly traded permanent capital platform signals interest in longer-term technology investments. David Cote (former Honeywell CEO) serves as GPGI Executive Chairman and controls a $1B equity stake. New CEO **Robert Domodossola** was appointed March 2, 2026 — a 30-year Husky veteran with an engineering background (VP Engineering career path).

**Data profile: Among the richest in the industry.** Husky possesses decades of parametric hot runner/mold design data from hundreds of engineers, real-time IoT data from **1,000+ connected machines across 75 countries** via their **Advantage+Elite™ platform** (built on Microsoft Azure IoT Hub, Azure Data Explorer, and Azure Synapse Analytics), configuration data from tens of thousands of annual quotes processed through **Tacton CPQ** (60-70 variables per solution, thousands of configuration rules), **Shotscope™ 4.0** plant monitoring capturing up to 250 process variables per machine, and decades of melt flow/thermal analysis data. The Tacton integration generates 95%+ of engineering BOMs automatically, indicating highly structured, parameterized design data.

**Digital/AI maturity: Significant infrastructure, limited AI deployment.** Advantage+Elite monitors 70+ performance parameters in real time across its connected base. Shotscope 4.0 provides silo-to-parts-handling data. Their Luxembourg manufacturing facility won a **FEDIL Innovation Award for Industry 4.0** (Plate Line Automation with digital twin technology). However, these systems appear to use **traditional analytics and rules-based algorithms** rather than ML/AI for predictive capabilities. There is no evidence of formal AI/ML research programs, dedicated data science teams, or AI company partnerships beyond Microsoft Azure infrastructure. **This represents a significant opportunity** — Husky has the infrastructure and data but may lack advanced AI/ML capabilities.

### How these companies' data ecosystems connect

The data ecosystem maps cleanly onto the physical workflow of injection molding:

1. **Design phase:** DME/HASCO/Meusburger component CAD libraries → embedded in mold designer's CAD environment → define the building blocks of every mold
2. **Hot runner engineering:** Husky/Mold-Masters/INCOE thermal/flow design data → specifies how plastic moves through the mold → connects to component selection from Step 1
3. **Process setup and monitoring:** RJG CoPilot/Kistler ComoNeo/Priamus FILLCONTROL cavity pressure data → validates that the mold (Steps 1-2) is performing correctly → generates continuous operational data
4. **Production tracking:** Progressive CVe/ProFile/HASCO Mold Track → monitors mold lifecycle and health → connects cycle data to design decisions from Steps 1-2

**The alliance's ontology, if it maps to DME/HASCO component standards, bridges cleanly across all four layers.** Component data provides the structural vocabulary; hot runner data provides the flow physics; process monitoring provides the performance validation; and production tracking provides the lifecycle context. No single company owns all four layers — the alliance could be the integrator.
