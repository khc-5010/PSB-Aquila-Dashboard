# Wisconsin Plastics Prospect Pipeline: 39-Company Intelligence Report

**The Penn State Behrend–Aquila Industrial AI Alliance has a deep, high-quality prospect pool in Wisconsin.** Of 39 companies researched, **18 are confirmed converters or converter-toolmakers suitable for High Priority prospecting**, with at least 6 showing multiple simultaneous readiness signals (RJG adoption, PE acquisition windows, leadership transitions, medical device focus). Wisconsin's western corridor — from Somerset to Baldwin to Prescott — is especially dense with sophisticated medical molders. The strongest immediate opportunities are **Kaysun Corporation** (confirmed RJG CoPilot adopter), **MRPC** (PE acquisition October 2025), **iMARK Molding** (RJG Training Excellence Award), and **EVCO Plastics** (confirmed cavity-pressure monitoring, generational transition underway). Bemis Manufacturing, despite its captive reputation, operates a major contract molding division and has **19 CWP contacts** — the warmest existing relationship in the dataset.

## Wisconsin as a Tier 2 market: the data supports it

Wisconsin's plastics manufacturing cluster is deeper and more sophisticated than a surface scan suggests. The research identified **18 confirmed converters or converter-toolmakers**, with a pronounced concentration in three sub-regions. The **western corridor** (Somerset–Amery–Baldwin–Woodville–Osceola–Prescott) is one of the highest-density medical plastics manufacturing corridors in the United States, anchored by SMC Ltd, Phillips-Medisize, Nolato Contour, iMARK/Comar, and MPP Corp. The **Milwaukee metro ring** (Germantown, Sussex, Muskego, Franklin, Mequon, Butler) houses MGS, Sussex IM, Alpha Plastics, E-S Plastic Products, and MRPC. The **Fox Valley/Lakeshore** zone (Manitowoc, Green Bay, Sheboygan, Clintonville) includes Kaysun, Wisconsin Plastics, Polyfab, Bemis, and Deluxe Plastics.

The CWP contact database provides 84 warm connections across these companies, with Bemis (19), Nolato Contour/Contour (7), SMC Ltd/Scientific Molding Corp (8 combined), Moxness (6), Polyfab (5), and MGS (5) representing the warmest entry points. No direct Penn State Behrend alumni were identified at any company through web research, but the CWP training relationship is a substantive warm connection that differentiates PSB from cold-outreach competitors in this market.
