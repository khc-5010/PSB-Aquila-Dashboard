## NOTES

### Key Patterns Observed

1. **The "RJG without AI" signal is confirmed at Automation Plastics (Aurora, OH).** This is only the second confirmed instance after X-Cell Tool in Erie. APC also has the deepest CWP relationship (11 contacts) of any non-Erie company. This should be prioritized immediately.

2. **Two PE acquisitions create time-sensitive windows:** Currier Plastics (Sheridan Capital, Sept 2025 — 6-month window NOW) and Empire Precision (Kouza Capital, Oct 2023 — still within optimization window). Both have in-house tooling and medical device focus. PE-backed companies have budget and mandate for operational improvements.

3. **Tessy Plastics is a strategic-level target that transcends the normal prospect pipeline.** With facilities in Erie PA, Meadville PA, and 1,500 employees, they're not a "prospect" — they're a potential strategic partner who already has geographic presence in PSB's backyard. The Custom Tool acquisition was already flagged. This needs elevation to Amy/Duane.

4. **Toledo Molding & Die's bankruptcy creates both risk and opportunity.** 19 CWP contacts is extraordinary. The distressed situation means current engagement is inappropriate, but tracking the acquisition outcome could yield a perfect post-acquisition entry. New ownership + legacy data chaos + 71-year tool library = ideal scenario.

5. **Western PA micro-clusters identified:** Latrobe PA has three CWP companies (Carclo, Classic Industries, Westmoreland Plastics), and York PA has four (York Imperial, Custom Molds/Plastics, Eden Tool, KS Tooling). These clusters may be worth a coordinated regional approach.

6. **CWP contact count is the single best proxy for PSB relationship depth.** Companies with 5+ CWP contacts have sent multiple employees through PSB training programs over years, indicating sustained engagement and trust. The top 20 companies by CWP contacts should be the starting point for any outreach planning.
